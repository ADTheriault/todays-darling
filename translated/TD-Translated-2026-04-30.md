## Title: A chatterbox fried egg that talks too much.
## Date: 2026-04-30
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

I might, as it turns out, be something of a proverb lover. Proverbs come to mind often, and I find myself savoring them. Sure, there are proverbs that mean the exact opposite of each other, but that's no contradiction. "Haste makes waste" and "strike while the iron is hot" both exist. When you're rushing too much, by all means reach for "haste makes waste." When you're dragging your feet and can't get moving, say "strike while the iron is hot." They just need to serve as material for judgment or conviction in the moment. It all depends on the time and place. On knowing when and where. You wouldn't bring a simmered daikon radish from an oden pot on a school field trip, and you probably shouldn't be eating a steamed pork bun at a funeral parlor, either. Ah, I realize that was an analogy for absolutely nothing. Feel free to let it go in one ear and out the other.

My favorite proverb, at present, is "Even a round egg can be cut square." Admittedly, there's a slight problem with calling an egg "round." Do people generally think of eggs as round? Isn't it really the other way around, that the egg is the very definition of "egg-shaped"? But never mind that. Either way, if you cut it square, it's square. You can apply this idea to just about everything. Let's try cutting some eggs into squares, off the top of our heads.

A rainy day is perfect weather for trying out a new umbrella.

Inari sushi is categorized as sushi, but if you think about it differently, isn't it really a tofu dish? You take tofu, slice it thin, deep-fry it, simmer it in a sweet-savory sauce, and eat it with vinegared rice.

Becoming the best in the world means, "Wait, I can't become the best in the world anymore?" You've already shifted from becoming to being.

Coming up with things like this is all you need to do. Just try turning the egg into something else. A fried egg, a rolled omelet, scrambled eggs, savory egg custard, an omelet, a boiled egg, a soft-cooked hot spring egg. They're all the same egg, aren't they? Just by becoming something different, the number of people it delights grows.

Today, I'm very grateful I managed to write something out of this idea. Thank you, as always, for coming to Hobonichi. I've been a little stretched thin lately, so for now, I'm keeping things loose.
