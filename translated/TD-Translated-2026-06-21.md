## Title: I tried writing it like a diary.
## Date: 2026-06-21
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

In Kesennuma, I ran into just about everyone I know. Tatekawa Shinosuke's rakugo performance acted like a magnet, drawing people in and pulling them together. That's wonderful, really. That's what good content should be. The Great Buddhas in Nara and Kamakura serve the same purpose, like magnets. Mount Fuji is an enormous magnet too.

I'd only just been to Kesennuma around March 11th, so the people I saw hadn't changed much. Except one guy, the most macho, most genuinely nice man in all of Kesennuma, had changed his hairstyle a little. Gentle waves in his hair now. Looked pretty good. When I pointed at his hair and said, "Hey, did you change something?" he answered, as if he'd been waiting for someone to ask, "What can I say, I'm feeling flirtatious these days." The man's over sixty. What is he talking about? But that kind of exchange is what makes conversation fun.

That got me thinking. I haven't changed my hairstyle in ages. Well, actually it has changed, but what I mean is I haven't deliberately changed it. The thing is, I've been going to the same salon for about fifty years. The salon stays the same, but the person in charge of my hair changes. I'm a little embarrassed to say this, but each time, whoever happened to be the manager at the time would take over as my stylist. And since I always told each of them, "I'll leave it up to you," whenever my style shifted a little, that was why.

The salon started in Harajuku, then moved twice in Aoyama. All that time, my head found its way there practically with my eyes closed. Then, about twenty years ago, the last manager who'd been doing my hair decided to go independent and open his own place. I followed him, and that's where I've been ever since. It's very close to home, which I'm grateful for.

Maybe I should get "flirtatious" too and change my hairstyle? No, probably not. I like leaving it up to someone else.

This isn't about "magnets" anymore. It's about "flow." Rather than exerting strong force, there's a comfort in letting go, in moving with the current.

I know this reads like a game of word association, but the truth is, today my head is full of the World Cup again.

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi today, too. A diary. Who is it usually written for, I wonder.
