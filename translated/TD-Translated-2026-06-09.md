## Title: A world where you can find almost anything if you look
## Date: 2026-06-09
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

It's never the case that all one hundred people out of a hundred are thinking the same thing. Even something you feel sure about, if half the people in the world happen to agree with you, you can safely say, "Well, that's a pretty good number." You might be absolutely convinced that "when it comes to oden, it's all about the daikon," but how many people would actually nod along and say, "Yes, daikon"? And the same goes for eggs. Konjac might be practically synonymous with oden, but I don't think all that many people would name it as their personal number one. (Based on a highly informal neighborhood survey.) When it comes to the question "What do you think of when you think of oden?", there is simply no answer that everyone will unanimously agree on.

So if you go around brimming with confidence, convinced that "everyone loves eggs," and you actually put the question to people, you'll find yourself caught off guard by voices calling out "shirataki!" or "chikuwa!" Someone with a narrow mind might even get angry. "How outrageous!"

I keep saying this, but we really need to understand that it's absolutely impossible for a hundred people to all be thinking the same thing. You can't just declare, "Everyone must want this kind of world," or "This is how people think." Outlandish opinions, ideas you'd never expect, for better or worse, they absolutely exist out there in the world.

People often say that ego-searching, looking yourself up online, is bad for your mental health, and you should stop doing it. That's because it's essentially going out of your way to hunt for information that will hurt you. If you make the effort to poke around in every back alley and side street, you will always find what's already there, because it's always been there. And once you notice it, it can throw off your inner sense of balance. Getting swept up in a whirlpool of agreement and affirmation is dangerous too, but so is fixating on every dark stain on the wall.

We live in an age where you can go and see the thoughts of not just a hundred people, but a million, as many as you like, whenever you want. That might actually be a strange thing for human beings. Thank you for visiting Hobonichi again today. Knowing too much, wanting to know too much. It pays to be careful.
