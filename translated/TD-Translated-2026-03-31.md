## Title: Mr. volume zone.
## Date: 2026-03-31
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

The rest of the Hobonichi crew have taken to calling me "Mr. Volume Zone." This is because I keep losing the game we've been playing at Hobonichi for ages, called "Only and Lonely." The way it works is simple: everyone picks a number, and the person who picks the smallest number wins the prize, but only if they're the sole person who picked that number. The smallest number is, of course, one, and the next smallest is two. But if more than one person votes for the same number, they're all disqualified. So everyone tries to think of a small number that nobody else would choose, but it turns out that quite a few people often land on the same idea of "a small number nobody else would choose." This, naturally, is a spectacular miss. Not even a close call. People laugh at you. That cluster of people is what we call the "volume zone." A collection of hopelessly ordinary, unimaginative souls, you might say. And right there in the thick of it, more often than not, is yours truly.

Every single time, I swear I'm thinking seriously about it. I weigh every angle, and today, today is the day I'm going to nail it. I want to be that lone victor who breaks through. But no matter how hard I try, how serious I get, how bold I am, I miss. I end up belonging to the herd, clumped together with everyone else. They always laugh at me, and by now I already know the consolation speech by heart: "That's exactly why your work goes so well, you know. You just naturally think the way most people think."

That's what they tell me, but... is that really true? I mean, come on. The things I do in my actual work aren't exactly what the majority would naturally come up with, are they? I've been told more than a few times that my ideas are somewhat unique, or that I bring a fresh perspective. People have flattered me like that on multiple occasions. Isn't that precisely why I've been able to make a career of it? So where does that version of me go? And who is this Mr. Volume Zone?

I often describe myself as "nothing special," and I genuinely mean it. But I have a feeling that somewhere in this contradiction lies a kind of secret, or maybe a key, that I haven't quite figured out yet.

Thanks for visiting Hobonichi again today. But seriously, the people who are actually good at this game... what kind of people are they?
