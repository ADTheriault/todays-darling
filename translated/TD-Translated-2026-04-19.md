## Title: Good things are good. Things that aren't… well.
## Date: 2026-04-19
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

When I watch a movie someone recommended, or eat something someone said was delicious, and I find something good in it, or it really does taste wonderful, I naturally want to tell other people. "This part was really interesting," or "It was delicious in this particular way," or "I made this little discovery." It just comes out. I used to be a copywriter in advertising, so thinking about what makes something good is something I enjoy, and maybe even something I'm fairly good at. Sometimes even what looks like a flaw can turn out to be a charming virtue. Change your perspective or your angle, and you'll find all sorts of interesting things.

On the other hand, when something doesn't really do it for me, or when it just wasn't very good, I have to be careful about sharing that. It might be that my way of seeing things is the odd one. Or it could be a case where the creator says, "Making you feel that way was exactly what I intended." In other words, I think there are plenty of times when the honest truth is simply: I wasn't able to find the good in it.

And there's one more thing. Spotting what's wrong with something comes pretty naturally to people. (Laughs.) Human beings have this tendency to pick apart others' flaws because it makes them feel like they're standing a little higher. "Why do you see the speck in your brother's eye but fail to notice the beam in your own?" People have been saying that since biblical times.

Thinking all of this over, I decided that when something doesn't strike me as very good, rather than making a point of saying so, I'd simply say nothing. The slightly tricky part, though, is that "choosing not to say anything" and "having nothing to say because I never saw it or tasted it in the first place" look exactly the same from the outside. Both just come across as silence. That's obvious, of course. But I suppose this is the only way I can keep going about it.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi again today. Finding the good in things, you know, that's its own kind of pleasure.
