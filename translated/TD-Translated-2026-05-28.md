## Title: An old man buying tickets late at night. Me.
## Date: 2026-05-28
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

We have the word "impulse buy," and I suppose this is a variety of that. I'm the kind of person who impulsively buys tickets. Even for events I'd never had any connection to, once I get the feeling that I really ought to see something, I want to go. Back before the internet existed, I was showing up at all sorts of performances and games. Murata Chōji's retirement game at Kawasaki Stadium. Misora Hibari's "Phoenix Concert" at the Tokyo Dome. Pink Floyd at the Hakone Aphrodite. The game where Sadaharu Oh hit home run number 756. RC Succession's debut at the Hibiya Open-Air Concert Hall. I'd get a sudden urge and head out on my own. I suppose you'd call it a rubbernecker's instinct. I have plenty more "I was there" stories to brag about, but I'll stop before I turn into an old man rambling about the past.

Still, this feeling of "I need to see this" has only grown healthier now that you can buy tickets online, because it's become so much easier to act on. I bought tickets to see Kiyoshi Maekawa late one night, all by myself, and went. He spotted me in the audience, and we had a reunion for the first time in twenty years.

Yesterday's "Sayuri Ishikawa Pre-55th Anniversary Concert" was another one of those tickets I clicked on in the middle of the night. Sayuri Ishikawa is apparently close friends with Akiko Yano, and it seems the two of them have gone to baseball games together in the States. On top of that, I once happened to be seated next to her at a Michiko Shimizu show, so we exchanged greetings. But that was about it.

At the end of the day, she's the voice behind this karaoke standard and that karaoke standard. She's someone who has given the world one "classic" of Japanese popular song after another. And yet, it occurred to me that never once having experienced Sayuri Ishikawa live was a real waste. So, striking while the iron was hot, I made the reservation, and headed to NHK Hall with Y-shita, who I'm not even all that close to.

Long story very short: the singing, incredible. The voice, I surrender. I was deeply grateful to the version of myself who had quietly bought those tickets in the middle of the night.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi today, too. A live performance is something you can never get back once you've missed it. Impulse is the best.
