## Title: Continuing from yesterday. About my favorite foods. A thank-you.
## Date: 2026-07-24
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

First of all, thank you all for your help.

Yesterday, right here, I put out a call: "Please send me an email about your favorite foods." What are your own favorite foods? Pick just three and write them down and send them to me. I asked with the feeling of running a little experiment, curious how many replies would come. These days, after all, emails have grown so rare.

But maybe it was because I'd written, a bit forlornly, "I usually only get about three," or maybe everyone poured in a little wish to cheer me on, because this time about a hundred times the usual number of emails arrived. Many people wrote, "I read you all the time." Honestly, that made me so happy. I'm the sort who takes real joy in that kind of thing.

And then, on top of all that, the contents turned out to be fascinating! One person, subjectivity on full display, declares "this is what I love." And they weigh which three to choose. Of course there are foods that everyone can imagine, but because this wasn't a multiple-choice survey, the answers were truly all over the place, full of individuality. Since these were the three that survived after dropping the fourth and fifth candidates, you can faintly sense the traces of someone turning it over in their mind. What you get is to stand there and let "the human heart," that thing that can't be tidily summed up, wash over you like a shower, just as it is.

"What is it that I actually love?" It strikes me that we don't often get the chance to ask ourselves that question. Maybe it's because no one asks us, or maybe it's because we simply don't think about it. But asking yourself questions of your own is, I think, something well worth doing more and more. There's a question, you want to answer it, so you start to think, to remember. And right there, your very self (none other than you) rises up like a hologram.

What first gave me the idea to try this was my daughter, who had written out a whole list of her own daughter's "favorite foods" in her Hobonichi Techo. And her daughter herself had added "croquette" there in her own handwriting. That page, with its row of favorite foods, looked to me like "this child's self-portrait."

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi again today. Today I'm heading out toward Iga. I'll be taking my parasol along.
