## Title: However, "three minutes" is a lot.
## Date: 2026-04-17
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

There's a proverb that goes, "Even a thief has three parts justice."

It's a pretty tough saying, but one most people know. Still, if you think about it a little more carefully, doesn't "three parts justice" seem like an awfully generous portion? We're talking seven to three here. Did you really think a thief had that much going for him? I certainly didn't. I'd always assumed it was one part, at most. So when I actually sat down and wrote it out, I was startled. Three parts?!

The reason I was writing it out in the first place was that I'd been about to start an essay on the theme of how even with people you really dislike, people you'd rather avoid entirely, there's usually something in what they say that you can partly agree with.

I'll be honest: there are people I don't like. There are plenty of times I think, "What an unpleasant way to see things." But even with those people, there are moments when I find myself thinking, "Well, actually, on that particular point, they might be right," or "I suppose I can see why they'd think that." Sometimes I can even genuinely agree. It's surprisingly hard to find a way of thinking you can reject wholesale. That's exactly why even harsh criticism can carry a grain of reason or feeling. The trouble is that you sort of get it, and that's what makes it so hard to dismiss.

So I was about to write along those lines, and I started by jotting down the familiar proverb about the thief, when it hit me: this isn't just "a little bit." It's three whole parts! That's serious. If the other side has three parts, doesn't that make our seven parts feel awfully precarious?

What I'd really wanted to say was that even our own thinking can't be entirely correct, that we're all "a little bit wrong from the start." But at a ratio of seven to three, I can't even get that argument off the ground.

So I think I'll go on living for a while longer with the image of "even a thief has one part justice" instead.

Come to think of it, there's another proverb: "Even a tiny insect has five parts spirit." That's not just a little. That's half. Half is spirit! The lesson, I suppose, is: never underestimate anything!
