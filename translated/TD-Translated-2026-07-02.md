## Title: The world is full of art.
## Date: 2026-07-02
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Okamoto Taro, when asked "What is art?", answered: it's that feeling of "What on earth is this?!" I've always loved that answer, and I've kept it tucked away in a corner of my mind.

There are so many "What on earth is this?!" moments. For a newborn baby, everything it sees, hears, and touches must be one big "What on earth is this?!" Anything that lies beyond what you can imagine, anything not yet named, all of it is "What on earth is this?!"

In other words, the moment a person feels "What on earth is this?!", their world has expanded by exactly that much. And that, Okamoto Taro went ahead and brilliantly declared, is the answer to "What is art?"

If "What on earth is this?!" is what art is, then nearly everything people have ever wondered about, asking "Is that art? Is this art?", ends up being art. Naturally, some people will object. Especially those who believe art must carry "a value deserving of reverence" or "commercial value." If anything and everything gets recognized as art, that's a real problem for them.

But let me say this on Okamoto Taro's behalf (forgive me!): what if we simply accepted that there can be "art that doesn't deserve reverence" and "art with no commercial value"? Wouldn't that settle it?

From ancient times right up to our present age of AI, the popular music that people poured their hearts into creating, the packaging design on everyday products, all of it is art. Just like grand temple architecture or portraits of kings, a great rock song, a gymnast's routine, the laughter drawn out by a comedy act, anything that makes someone feel "What on earth is this?!" is art. And among all of it, some things will endure for a thousand years.

Thanks for coming to Hobonichi again today. Even an amateur's karaoke is art. Not that anyone's going to praise it.
