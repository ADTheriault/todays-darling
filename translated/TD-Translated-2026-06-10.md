## Title: There is no dog that doesn't play at all.
## Date: 2026-06-10
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Our dog Buiko is, for a dog, not very active. Don't get me wrong, she's adorable, but active she is not. She seems to like going outside, yet she's not fond of walks. She'll wander around the yard for a bit, then immediately take on an attitude of "Shall we go back in now?" Our previous dog, Bouillon, never once said "That's enough" no matter how long a walk lasted. It was always "More!" On top of that, even when she was inside, she was constantly bouncing around, looking for any chance to play. She'd grab a ball, trot over, and drop it right at my feet, demanding I throw it. (Iwata-san got roped into that one plenty of times.) When you threw it, she'd race after it, sprint back at full speed, and drop it again. Over and over, as many times as you'd let her. She was a dog who lived for play, for whom physical activity was the greatest joy. With the energy levels we have now, there's no way we could keep up with her.

It's not as though we chose our match based on that kind of convenience, but the energy-saving lifestyle of our second dog has been a real blessing. The only time she gets active is when she's "reporting for duty" to announce that someone has come to the front door. That's the one occasion when she barks her heart out. You always do good work, Buiko. Thank you.

Now, even a dog like our second one will, every once in a while, come over to invite me to play. She'll position herself in my line of sight and let out a quiet little grumble. When I respond to the signal and stand up, she runs ahead to where the toys are kept, picks up a rubber ball, and sets it down on the spot. I throw it and she goes after it, but she doesn't bring it back. She just stays where she is, waiting for me to walk over to her. There's the "go" of back-and-forth, but no "back." So I have no choice but to go to her. The dog simply waits. From wherever she's standing, I pick up the ball and throw it again. She chases it, then stops where it lands. After a few rounds of this, she stops chasing the ball altogether. A dog, not chasing a ball. And that's the end of it, just a few minutes in all.

But the interesting thing is, it's not that she doesn't want to play. Well, say what you will, I suppose the dog in her blood is still there after all.

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi today. Bouillon, Buiko, and I: none of us could go through life without play.
