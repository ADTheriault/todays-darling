## Title: Something resembling courage.
## Date: 2026-04-25
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

The things you do because you want to do them will always surpass the things you do because you have to. Even if you're not trying to surpass anything, they just do. And even if they didn't, you wanted to do it, so you spent your time well. That's something I've been thinking about a lot lately.

I've thought along these lines before, but I was always a little short on something like courage. I'd hold back on asking myself whether I really wanted to do something, and instead I'd mix in a dose of "I have to, so I will." I know, I know. There are things that have to be done. Maybe I've spent my whole life just practicing that, just carrying it out over and over. But honestly, that kind of thing doesn't make your heart tremble. Not your own heart, and probably not anyone else's either.

The other day, writing for a certain event, I put down, half-jokingly, "I'll do this even if nobody shows up." And I felt an incredible rush of exhilaration. "I'll do this even if nobody shows up. Because I want to." I thought that feeling was something very close to courage.

If you're always wishing "please let tons of people come," then even falling just a little short of tons will probably leave you disappointed, unable to feel satisfied. Wait, wasn't this something you wanted to do? If you genuinely believe "I'll do this even if nobody comes," then when two or three people show up, you're overjoyed. And on top of that, you got to do the thing you wanted to do.

Just being able to do what you want to do is already a perfect score. Just having the chance to do it at all is something incredible. And to a place like that, whoever's meant to come will come.
