## Title: Hey peaches and blossoms, let's have a little chat.
## Date: 2026-03-03
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Some people probably noticed, but those who forgot don't even realize they've forgotten. Today is March third, that famous "Peach Blossom Festival." It's the main day of Hinamatsuri, when you display hina dolls and eat scattered sushi, hina rice crackers, diamond-shaped mochi, and sweet white sake. Come to think of it, yes, that's right. I have the knowledge, the memories. But surprisingly, everyone just lets it pass by. I suppose it's different for families with little girls or young daughters.

I tried to think about why it's become so forgotten. One reason, I think, is that we don't see TV commercials for hina dolls anymore. Probably hina doll sales aren't big enough to support all those ads like in the old days. For one thing, everyone's homes are smaller now. Once you decide that just the top-tier emperor and empress dolls are enough, you don't need the whole entourage of three court ladies and five musicians. And of course you won't buy all the accompanying furnishings and furniture either. Taking them out and putting them away is such a hassle, after all. It's becoming "quietly kept among family," just like how funerals have shifted to small family services. In other words, it's no longer a festival in everyone's consciousness.

And then there are the peaches. Don't forget, it's the Peach Blossom Festival. But have you actually seen peach blossoms blooming in your neighborhood? Unless you live in a peach-growing region like Yamanashi or Okayama, I don't remember ever being conscious of peach blossoms blooming. I don't really know the difference between plum, peach, and cherry blossoms either, to be honest. I do know they're all part of the rose family, though. In just a little while, the cherry blossoms will start blooming and steal all the attention anyway. Even though everyone pays such attention to peaches when it comes time to eat them.

But this year I remembered. Not that it matters much, but here I am, someone who lives ignoring Valentine's Day and lucky direction sushi rolls, remembering the Peach Blossom Festival and sighing, "I'd forgotten all about it." Peaches, blossoms, I'm sorry, but I have nothing more to say about you. If I remember again next year, I'd like to talk about the same sort of thing.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi today. The cherry blossoms will probably be in full bloom again sometime this month, won't they.
