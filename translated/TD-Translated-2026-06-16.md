## Title: There has never been a draw so universally praised as this one.
## Date: 2026-06-16
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Sorry about all the comings and goings yesterday. In the end, I managed to watch the World Cup match on TV, full of excitement. When you think about it, what a convenient world we live in now. Here's a soccer match being played in Texas, in the United States. A team that traveled all the way from the Netherlands, and a team on the road from Japan, where we live, facing off against each other, and at that very same moment, an ordinary citizen like me is sitting here watching it. When it comes to things like this, I'm genuinely in awe of the progress of civilization. Relay runners and smoke signals wouldn't have cut it, and even with postal mail or telephones, you could never enjoy a live broadcast. Friends of mine tell me that even deep in the mountains of Nepal, the local women all have smartphones now. When it comes to the act of "communicating," the world has already reached a point of no return.

You might think it's strange that I'm marveling at all this now. "Why is he going on about something so obvious?" But let me have my moment of wonder, please. I'm a person from another era, originally. I was a kid who drank well water. If you like, I could tell you all about pit latrines. A single charcoal brazier in a freezing classroom in the dead of winter. When I think back on it now, I knew an age that was practically something out of a period drama, and I also know the world as it is around 2026. Depending on how you look at it, that might actually be a tremendous privilege.

Even so, the afterglow of the soccer match lingers, and I keep snacking on bits of news from that world. There's been a sudden flood of stories about "how incredible Japan is." It seems all sorts of people from other countries have been breaking down the strategy that Manager Moriyasu had envisioned. I don't have the sharpest eye for understanding these things, but as an amateur, I've been reading it all with real fascination. And among everything I've seen, the fact that Moriyasu was giving tactical instructions during the match by scribbling numbers on a whiteboard by hand. Honestly, I absolutely loved that. It was the best!

Thanks for coming to Hobonichi again today. I'm in trouble, though. My brain just won't shift back to its regular schedule.
