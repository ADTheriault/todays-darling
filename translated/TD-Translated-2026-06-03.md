## Title: I picked up a nice "thing I don't have to do."
## Date: 2026-06-03
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

When I write, I almost never use quotations. I say "almost," so it's not that I never do, but I tend to avoid them. They're a hassle. Basically, I like to make do with whatever happens to be lying around. If I wanted to sound like an intellectual about it, I'd call it "something like bricolage." So when there's some impressive quote I could pull out, well, that's not exactly "making do with what's at hand," is it? It falls squarely into the category of things I'm bad at, things that feel like too much trouble. I was just about to write something along those lines when I stumbled upon a piece of text I was dying to quote. And what's more, it was just "lying around."

Sugano: "The things you have to do? You don't have to do them." (Repeats the same line three times.)

Nagata, who happened to be there: "Exactly. The things you have to do, there's only so interesting they can get."

Sugano: "Wait, what? Say that again, word for word."

Nagata: "The things you have to do, there's only so interesting they can get. But the things you don't have to do? There's no limit to how interesting those can be. Because, well, you don't have to do them."

What a magnificent conversation. It's from yesterday's live text coverage at the Hobonichi "Joys of Everyday Life" exhibition. If you find something like this just lying on the ground, of course you're going to pick it up. I'm swiping it. I'm not turning it in at the lost-and-found. I'm copying it down, just like that. Part quotation, part finders-keepers, part "something like bricolage."

Yes. The things you do out of obligation, because you feel like you have to, the outcome is predictable and it never gets interesting. The Hobonichi people who were having this conversation are out in Shinjuku again today, searching for things they don't have to do.

Thank you, as always, for visiting Hobonichi today. How are things where you are after the typhoon? I wonder what's in store for all of us.
