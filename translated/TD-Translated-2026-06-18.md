## Title: I want to open the windows and widen the doorways.
## Date: 2026-06-18
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Hot during the day, then surprisingly chilly once night falls. And if you're not paying attention, the rain sneaks up on you too. The rainy season keeps you on your toes. But as long as it's not one of those endless stretches of downpour, I don't really mind it. The green after a good soaking is truly beautiful, so I'm actually rather fond of this time of year. That said, I've been getting my parasol ready for the relentless heat of midsummer, which will be upon us before long.

Something I've been thinking about lately: no matter what the weather is like, just being able to open a window now and then feels like such a gift. In a lot of newer office buildings, even ones with perfectly good climate control, the windows don't open. And of course I understand why. Someone could fall. It's dangerous. But spaces with no windows, no connection to the outside, they've never sat well with me.

Sure, you can control the temperature, the humidity, the air quality. But I think something in us, something unconscious, is always registering: this place is sealed off. Because it is. You really are in a closed space, when it comes down to it. I can't help thinking that has an effect on the people inside. Any living creature, packed into a small, sealed space with others, things aren't going to go well. And it's not just a matter of ventilation.

Being able to sense that flow between inside and outside, that openness, I think that's one of the most important conditions for feeling comfortable in a place. You can eat and sleep in a prison, too. But the fact that you can't leave, that you're in a closed space, that alone is punishment enough.

Freedom to come and go. Openness. If you don't keep that consciously in mind, things tend to close up on their own. Closed systems are easier to control, after all. And it's much the same in virtual spaces online. That feeling of things closing in, people colliding, the air growing stifling.

Thanks for visiting Hobonichi today. My ideal is something like an engawa, that in-between space, half inside and half outside, where the two can meet.
