## Title: Here's what people who can't sleep should try, apparently.
## Date: 2026-07-12
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

I like finding a hypothesis, a little "maybe it's like this?", and then spending a while turning it over in my mind and testing it out.

Right now, to put it broadly, I've been thinking about how "your subjective take matters" far more than I ever imagined. Even when the exact same thing happens, everything can veer off in a completely different direction depending on the "subjectivity" of the person taking it in. The facts are identical, the cast of characters, the time, the place, all of it the same, and yet depending on "how you feel about it, how you think about it," you end up connected to an utterly, utterly different future.

Someone who can hold a "nice subjectivity" probably moves closer to happiness, while someone with the habit of a "bad subjectivity" seems bound for a rough time. Of course, I'm no fortune-teller, so I can't say for certain. But I get the feeling there might be some kind of training you could do to strengthen that "nice subjectivity." The same way you can train up your "core" muscles. And so, at the moment, this is the idea that's all the rage inside my head.

Now then, one thing I've been experimenting with pretty much every day is the practice of falling asleep without taking a "sleeping aid." Like most old folks, at a certain point I got bad at sleeping. Getting into bed and not being able to drift off, waking up over and over, these unwelcome tendencies had been with me for ages, so I took the quick route and started using "medicine that makes it easier to fall asleep." With it, sleep came easily, and I got hooked.

But then I learned that fretting too much over "not being able to sleep" is itself the problem, that simply lying down with your eyes closed already counts as rest, and I decided to try, as much as I could, to think of it that way. That's not to say the feeling of "I'd hate it if I couldn't sleep" has been wiped away entirely, mind you.

I made myself believe, subjectively, that "even if I can't sleep, it's fine," and I get into bed with enough ease to think, if I still can't sleep after a while, I'll figure out my next move then. And so, it's not that I drop off instantly, but before long, sleep comes.

I warm up in the bath, and I go to bed earlier than I used to. And I don't let the sleeplessness get to me. I just think, "Come on, bring it." With this, somehow, it's been going well for nearly a month now. I'd like to keep it up until I hit a wall or simply get bored of it.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi today too. A "nice subjectivity" really might be something you can pick up with a little practice, don't you think?
