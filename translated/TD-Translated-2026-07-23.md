## Title: Please send me an email about your favorite food.
## Date: 2026-07-23
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Today, I thought I'd try a little experiment.

I'm sure you've all felt it for ages now, but the way we communicate online these days has changed quite a bit from how it used to be. Something new pops up, this or that catches on for a while, one place feels like it's descended into chaos, another you've never even heard of. Me, for instance, I hardly use the phone anymore. And even "email," which I once relied on so gladly, I find myself using less and less.

Every day I write these short pieces, and I get maybe three emails with any thoughts on them. I honestly start to worry whether anyone's reading at all. But as you well know, the policy here is "we'll do it even if not a single customer shows up," so I have no intention of stopping. Every night I think, and every night I write.

And so, though it's a bit late in the day for this, I found myself wanting to give something a try. In a situation like this, if I actually came out and asked, just how many emails would come back to me?

I'm not asking you to send me your impressions of my humble little essays. Just write down three of your favorite foods and email them to me. The address is the same as always, postman@1101.com.

A single line is fine: "Ramen, watermelon, natto." But let's set one condition. Think it over carefully, "What is it that I actually like?" and be sure to write down exactly three. Then send it to this "place" that everyone at Hobonichi and I get to read.

It shouldn't be much trouble, but let's use this chance to think about "what does three really mean?" If you feel like writing about how you love them, go right ahead. I think mulling this over is good for your mental health, too.

Favorite foods, after all, are a matter of personal taste. And even if you commit firmly to your own subjective picks and share them, no one can complain. There aren't many chances to state your own preferences so proudly, and it becomes a moment to face yourself anew: "Ah, so this is the person who loves these three things."

As a rule, let's say two or five won't do. That's all. To everyone who's read this far, I'll be waiting for your emails.

Thank you, as ever, for coming to Hobonichi today. And as always, I read every single email that arrives.
