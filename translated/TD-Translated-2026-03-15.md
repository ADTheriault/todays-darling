## Title: It was 10,000 times, apparently.
## Date: 2026-03-15
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Someone at Hobonichi seems to have noticed. Those short pieces I've been writing all this time, hadn't they quietly passed the ten-thousand mark? Well, it's not like I've been counting as I go. Every year when June 6th comes around, I'm aware of how many years it's been. But as for the act of writing every day, I've made that the subject here more than a few times. People are often impressed that I've kept it up so long, which makes it an easy thing to write about. "It must be so hard," they say, or "Aren't there days you just don't want to write?" That kind of reaction is exactly what I was hoping for when I started.

When I launched Hobonichi, I thought the one thing even I could do, if I just committed to it, that would make people happy, was to write every single day without fail. I wanted to be like a shopkeeper who shows up at the store every day. Whether it's good or bad, polished or rough, I can't really judge. In cooking terms, "as long as it's not terrible, that's enough." (I'm stealing that from Yoshiharu Doi.) There's no trick to keeping it going. You decide to do it, and you do it. It truly is something anyone can do if they just do it, but people are kind enough to say, "That's exactly the thing most people can't do."

To keep going every day without a break, well, you try not to get seriously ill, you think of ways to manage when things get desperate. As an extension of simply being alive, it takes a little ingenuity. But as far as effort goes, it feels like a pretty cheap investment.

So, once I heard it might have passed ten thousand, I got curious about when exactly that had been, and I asked ChatGPT. It told me the ten-thousandth one fell on October 21, 2025. And while it was at it, today's installment is apparently number 10,145.

Well, after writing ten thousand of these, pretty much all of my foolishness and failings and sloppiness have been laid bare. That might actually be the best thing to come out of it.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi today, too. Doing what you can do if you just do it turns out to be, well, surprisingly doable.
