## Title: Olivia, it's not going to be a big deal.
## Date: 2026-04-28
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Now then.

Are ears hair? No. Is the area behind the ears hair? No. Is the nape of the neck hair? No matter how much allure it exudes, it is not. The neck isn't hair either, and the forehead isn't. None of them are hair. So I have no intention of washing them with shampoo, which is meant for hair. That said, the hairline along the forehead and the area behind the ears do get washed with shampoo as part of the whole flow of things. I'm sure everyone does the same.

Hair gets washed with shampoo, the face gets washed with some kind of face-washing foam, and everything else, the body in general, gets washed with body wash. That's how it's supposed to go. It's better that way, so that's what we do.

But. If you washed your face with hair shampoo, or scrubbed your chest or stomach or other parts of your body with it, would something terrible happen? Probably not. For that matter, you could do your laundry with it or clean the bathtub, and nothing terrible would happen.

I may sound like I'm belaboring the point here, but isn't that just how it is, folks? Shampoo, face wash, body wash. They come in separate bottles, so we use them accordingly. But even if you don't strictly follow those divisions, most of the time, nothing terrible is going to happen.

This time, unusually, I already knew what I wanted to write about. It's this: "Nothing terrible is going to happen." That's what I wanted to say, and the shampoo talk was just my way of getting there.

Sure, there are things that truly do become terrible. Plenty of them. Matters of life and death, things that shake the world. I can't speak to those. I don't know enough. But there are also heaps of things on the level of which body part you wash with shampoo. Piles and piles of not-terrible things. For those, it's fine to just watch the pitches go by and strike out looking.

And of course, even if you hadn't read this essay, nothing terrible would have happened!

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi again today. Even when nothing terrible is at stake, there are still things worth caring about.
