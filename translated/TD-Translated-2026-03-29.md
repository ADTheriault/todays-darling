## Title: The grace of being accepted.
## Date: 2026-03-29
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Around the time I was turning thirty, maybe just before or just after, I remember Eikichi Yazawa saying something like this: "People talk about kids going off the rails, but really, they've lost their way. They don't know which direction to go, where they're supposed to be. They're just lost." I have no idea if that's etymologically correct, but it was so convincing that I've found myself coming back to it again and again.

If you have even one place you can return to, one place where you belong, if you haven't truly lost your way, then apparently, more often than not, you can find a way to start over from there. I've also heard that when a kid who's gone astray finds no one willing to take them in, sometimes the only ones who welcome them warmly and sweetly are the genuinely dangerous organizations. Being accepted. That's an enormous thing.

There are all kinds of flocks out there in the world. Each has its good points, its quirks, and yes, some are groups that actually do bad things. If you wandered into a gathering you had no connection to, you might come away saying, "All they ever talked about was such-and-such, and it was unbearable." But within that flock, talking about nothing but such-and-such is probably the most natural thing in the world.

They say birds of a feather flock together, and the birds that do flock together must be accepting one another. Where can a bird like me find acceptance? What kind of bird would this flock be willing to take in? Maybe that exchange is quietly playing out all the time, over and over.

I've gone through life without thinking too hard about where I fit. But looking back, so many different people have welcomed me in, and I'm filled with genuine gratitude. It means I wasn't rejected. That alone is something to be deeply thankful for. Not being rejected. Having a place to be. Maybe even being understood. These things are as essential as water and air.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi today, too. When you have the security of knowing you're accepted, you can even be alone.
