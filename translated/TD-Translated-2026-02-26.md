## Title: You can assume that personality doesn't change.
## Date: 2026-02-26
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

I think it's difficult to change your personality. Looking at all kinds of people, and at myself, I've come to feel deeply that personality just doesn't change. However, regardless of personality, you can change your behavior and attitude.

Those who know me probably know this already, but I am, for example, a shy person. The feeling of embarrassment is very strong in me. When I was a child, people close to me would say this about me, but nowadays they don't really say it anymore, and they don't seem to think of me that way either. But I know the truth: I'm still shy. Still, I can avoid choosing behaviors that come from being shy, as much as possible. When it's okay to be shy, I can be unabashedly embarrassed. But in situations where it would be inappropriate to act shy, I behave like someone who isn't embarrassed at all. Well, when it comes to relationships with people or social roles, there are many situations where you can't afford to stay shy, so most of the time I end up adopting a non-shy attitude. I suppose it's a bit like changing clothes.

Even looking at dogs, each one has a completely different personality. Bouillon, a dog I used to have, disliked other dogs and would either become aggressive or avoid interaction. But Buiko, the dog I have now, loves dogs and will wag her tail and bound up to any dog without discrimination. I think humans probably have personality differences just as pronounced as this. You might as well decide that this is something you can't fix even if you try, like the length of your arms or legs. But your attitude and behavior don't have to match your personality. Sure, since it's not coming from your core, it won't feel completely natural. But yes, you can change your behavior like changing clothes. In fact, I hear that people who work in comedy often operate this way. You could say it's better not to force yourself, but I think changing like you'd change clothes is perfectly fine.

Thanks for coming to Hobonichi again today. If I'd stayed shy all this time, I wouldn't even be able to write essays.
