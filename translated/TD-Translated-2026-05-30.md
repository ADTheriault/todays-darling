## Title: Flowers are still blooming.
## Date: 2026-05-30
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

They say the flower of Japan is the cherry blossom. Whenever Japan comes up, it's always cherry blossoms, as if this were a country where nothing else blooms. And sure, cherry blossoms have that big-star quality about them. But listen.

After the cherry blossoms have scattered, down around knee height as you walk, azaleas burst into vivid color. There are so many of them, blooming everywhere, that nobody ever bothers to have an azalea-viewing party. Even kids today, just like kids from long ago, pluck the azalea flowers and sip the nectar from them. "So sweet!" they say, same as children always have.

Before long, in hedges and garden beds all over town, roses start to bloom. Roses are so popular, and flower shops carry them in every season, so it's hard to notice when they actually start blooming somewhere on the vine. But right around now is the perfect time for planted roses to flower. It's not just the magnificent blossoms worthy of a shop display. The modest, unremarkable ones are lovely too.

Right around the time you start thinking about the rainy season, the plum trees bear fruit. Oh, right, you remember. The Japanese word for the rainy season is written with the characters for "plum" and "rain." The plum blossoms themselves came back in the cold of February, didn't they? Then, depending on where you are, apricots start ripening too. I love apricots. This year I'll almost certainly make apricot jam myself again. Of all the jams in the world, apricot is my favorite.

By the end of the rainy season it's already summer, and all manner of weeds are stretching and spreading with wild abandon. They might be a nuisance, but I envy that kind of energy. And yes, morning glories send their tendrils climbing and open their flowers everywhere. Sunflowers stand tall in the city and the countryside alike. Being big is a perfectly fine thing to be known for, sunflowers.

They say the flower of Japan is the cherry blossom, but the flower calendar keeps going long after the blossoms fall. In every season, actors who may not be stars but who know their craft are blooming out there, whether we bother to look or not. Just as there is always music somewhere, flowers are blooming right now.

Thank you, as always, for coming to Hobonichi today. And my sincere gratitude to everyone who tends the plantings along our streets.
