## Title: A trivial tale of dangling shoelaces.
## Date: 2026-04-24
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

"Hobo no Eki AKAGI" opens its doors today, at long last. Yesterday we held a preview for people involved in the project and members of the media. The curtain is finally going up. Between the live text updates from the scene and everything else, I'm sure plenty of information has already reached you, but let me share a little behind-the-scenes story of my own from the site.

Opening day! A grand opening means a purification ceremony, of course. And when you say Akagi, you mean Akagi Shrine. (The history of this shrine is extraordinary. Apparently it goes back twelve hundred years.) They graciously came out to perform the rites at Hobo no Eki AKAGI.

I've been through Shinto ceremonies like this any number of times, but every single time I find myself thinking, "Wait, how does this go again?" These rituals aren't something you can just stand and watch. There's a ceremonial offering of the tamagushi, the sacred branch. And at some point along the way, I ended up being the one who always goes first. Which means I can't just copy the person ahead of me and muddle through.

I have fragments of knowledge. "Rotate the sakaki branch to the right." "Two bows, two claps, one bow." But I am never, ever confident about it. I mean, you know me. I'm the kind of man who's hopeless with procedures of any kind. Still, I steeled myself. I'll do my best, give it everything I've got, first this, then that... I braced myself and waited for my name to be called.

Now, when my turn actually came, I did know the part about turning the sakaki branch to face the deity. That much I had down. But the stand, the thing you place the tamagushi on, it wasn't right in front of me! Given where it was, it looked like I'd have to step out of my shoes and go up to it. And there was no way I could just slip my shoes off that easily, especially not while holding a sakaki branch. So I decided to loosen my laces ahead of time. That way I could get them on and off smoothly, whether stepping up or stepping back.

I was just in the middle of secretly loosening the laces so they'd come off easily when my name was called. And the stand, the thing you place the tamagushi on, was carried over from the back and set right in front of me.

Look, I really don't think it was a big deal. But there I was, receiving the sacred tamagushi branch with both shoelaces dangling loose. It's the kind of trivial thing that would never make it into the live text coverage. Just a little behind-the-scenes moment from the ceremony, witnessed only by those sitting in the front row.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi today. Here's to the grand opening of Hobo no Eki AKAGI. Let's make it fun, let's make it lively, let's go!
