## Title: On hot things.
## Date: 2026-03-02
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

A thought occurred to me. Well, actually, thoughts are always occurring to me, but here's one: don't people think a bit too highly of "passion"? He's got fire in his heart, I felt his passion, don't lose that fire, what passionate love... All of these are fine, really, I think they're fine. Right up until the point where we "think too highly" of them. If it's just "nice to have some fire," that's probably okay.

But if you really think about it, you'll see what I mean. "Fire" is good for a short period, but constantly burning without a break is a problem, isn't it? Even a protagonist burning with passion is only "great" because in a drama you part ways after an hour or so. If they followed you home like that, you'd be in trouble, wouldn't you? "I'm on fire!" said at the dinner table, I mean, come on.

Even when cooking rice, you turn up the heat until it boils. Once it boils over, you turn the flame down to minimum for ten minutes. Depending on which school of earthenware pot you follow, some people turn off the heat entirely. Nobody does anything like "I'm on fire!" After that, there's time to let it steam, and then you transfer it to the rice container.

I love anmitsu, so I often make kanten. Of course you put it in water and boil it, but if you don't let it cool, it won't become kanten. And then you serve it chilled below room temperature, right?

Work, human relationships, any kind of training or practice, I think it's all like this. You can't try to keep running at peak "passion." It might seem like a good idea, but that's not how things work. "Passion" and "warmth," "lukewarm" and "cool," you need to manage all these different temperatures.

Well, the big orientation session for "Hobonichi Manga Club" was so good that I thought, I'd better be careful not to get "too heated up" about this. Though honestly, "passion" has never really been my strong suit anyway.

Thanks for coming to Hobonichi today. When you get caught up in the heat, sometimes you can trip over your own emotions.
