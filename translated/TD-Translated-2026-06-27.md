## Title: How far down do you eat a watermelon?
## Date: 2026-06-27
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

I'm pretty fond of watermelon, and I eat it often. At some point I realized that, somewhere along the way into adulthood, I'd stopped eating the part near the rind where it isn't sweet anymore. I don't scrape at the white part, of course, but even within the red part, I leave behind whatever isn't sweet.

If the childhood version of me saw this, I wonder what he'd think. "There's still good watermelon left!" he'd surely say. Because that kid ate every bit of it. I still remember the feeling. The red part is edible, and it should be eaten. The proudest way to eat watermelon was to keep going until not a trace of red remained. Who exactly I was showing off for, I'm not sure.

But the truth is, the part near the rind just doesn't taste good. Maybe some people like it, but it's certainly not sweet. As a kid, it didn't matter, but as an adult, I came to feel that forcing yourself to eat the parts you don't enjoy actually ruins the memory of all the delicious bites that came before. You've been savoring this sweet, wonderful watermelon, and then the grand finale is bland, tasteless rind? That felt wrong.

At some point, without quite realizing it, I started thinking this way, and I changed how I ate watermelon. It's not a matter of "eat the red, leave the white." That's not how I see it anymore. It's about sweetness, not color. I made that decision for myself.

The same thing applies to melon, if I'm being honest. With melon, the colors are less distinct, so I just stop at the point where I judge it's no longer sweet.

I've never looked into how far other people eat their watermelon or melon, so I couldn't say. And nobody's ever scolded me for leaving red watermelon behind. While I'm at it, I should mention that I stopped drinking ramen broth to the last drop a long time ago, even though the broth is the most expensive part of the bowl. I imagine there are people who'd have something to say about that.

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi again today. Whether this makes me stingy or extravagant, I honestly can't tell myself.
