## Title: Peace without words.
## Date: 2026-05-22
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

You've probably seen this kind of scene before, at a daycare, a nursery, a park. Tiny children, barely more than babies, playing together, looking happy. Well, they're not always getting along, and sometimes they seem to be ignoring each other entirely. So maybe "playing together" is a stretch. Oh, and dogs at a dog park are the same way. Some are chasing each other around, some are off on their own, some are tumbling together. It's all sorts of things. But either way, in a space that isn't all that big, there's this scene of creatures naturally gathered together. Nobody's in charge. There's no apparent purpose. And there are no "words" to speak of (well, okay, maybe some babbling, some barking). Each of them has their own hunger, their own sleepiness, their own little quarrels. They're all separate individuals, separate dogs. And yet, somehow, they're just naturally there together.

I like watching that kind of thing. And maybe it's not just me. Maybe humans in general like watching it.

I know perfectly well that a world with purpose, with systems and rules, with scheming and strategy and tactics, can be fascinating as a game. That's why I watch dramas, why I watch sports. But there's something about "living creatures, simply being there together" that is its own kind of wonderful, its own kind of quietly heartwarming. I even feel something like longing when I see it.

Especially the part about having no "words." I envy that. I'm someone who has spent his life working with words, and I should know better than anyone how powerful and precious they are. And this very piece I'm writing right now is made of words. But maybe that's exactly why, maybe it's precisely because of that, that I find myself longing for silence.

Dogs and babies can speak with their eyes alone. Sometimes they leap at you, or climb into your arms. These wordless creatures are unbearably dear to me.

Thank you, as always, for coming to Hobonichi today. Words can be weapons, and they can be the red thread of fate that binds us. But so can silence.
