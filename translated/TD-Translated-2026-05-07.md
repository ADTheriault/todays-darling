## Title: The curious expressive form known as the memo.
## Date: 2026-05-07
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Someday, once I've thought it through a little more, I'll write about it. That's the kind of thing I jot down as a memo. And sometimes I really do think it through and write it up properly. But other times, even after thinking a little more, I find I can't write it after all, and the memo just sits there.

I'm not great at thinking things through rigorously, and sure, there's something like an inferiority complex in that. But lately I've started to wonder if maybe that's not really the issue.

Back when I was working as a copywriter, I wrote a line like "Delicious Life." Beyond that, I wrote a few lines of body copy, but it was hardly an essay, just a handful of words. If someone had told me at the time, "Explain it in more detail," would I have launched into some long-winded commentary on the copy? I was interviewed about that line a number of times, but I was never, ever able to say anything that truly satisfied the interviewer. And now I think that was just fine.

Think of haiku, or tanka, or short verse. Someone once wrote the poem, "The old pond, a frog jumps in, the sound of water." That poem doesn't need body copy. And there's certainly no need to sit down and develop it into a thesis. Still, if the author wanted to talk about those seventeen syllables, they could go on as long as they liked. I think that's how it works.

I'm fully aware this is self-serving, but even though a short memo isn't a recognized art form, I came to think, "Maybe it's fine just as it is." If you want to explain further what's written there, go ahead. And if you don't, just put it out into the world anyway.

Freedom, and how extraordinary a thing it is, can be understood from a single fact: the deprivation of freedom is what we use as punishment.

...That was a memo, set down just as it was. If I tried to continue, I think it would only get tedious.

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi again today. And come to think of it, I probably could have said even that more briefly, as a memo.
