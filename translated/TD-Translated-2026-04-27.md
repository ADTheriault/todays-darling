## Title: My idea of kayōkyoku is probably "songs for grown-ups"
## Date: 2026-04-27
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

The "Kayōkyoku Kissaten" event at Shibuya PARCO has finally opened its doors. It's a long run centered around Golden Week. I'll be there for two nights hosting my own sessions, one night as companion to a guest performer, and then several more times just because I personally want to listen. At this point, I'm practically a fixture of the place.

I haven't given any thought at all to what I'll actually say there. I trust that the charm contained in each and every song will give the listeners words of their own.

As for how to define kayōkyoku, I honestly don't have a clear answer, but, well, they're popular songs, aren't they? And I also think of them as "songs for grown-ups." Of course, there are kayōkyoku that are wholesome and uplifting and forward-looking, but even so, I feel like a song can't really qualify as kayōkyoku unless it's a "song for grown-ups." Even when a song is about the pure, innocent heart of a young woman, or the bold spirit of a young man trying to light up the world, it was still crafted as a "song for grown-ups."

For my own selections this time, I held back a little, but fundamentally I think I ended up gathering "songs for grown-ups." Not explicit, but singing about the adult heart. Listening to songs like these back in elementary school, feeling like you half-understood and half-didn't, I think that had an enormous influence on the person I later became.

The old kayōkyoku had a kind of darkness that seeped through. Something like a murky desire that drifted off the adults around you. When I was young, it honestly made me uncomfortable. There was a time when I wanted to get as far away from it as possible. But once you start to understand things, it gets interesting.

On that note, I've been thinking that maybe Anzen Chitai was the last great figure of that kayōkyoku world. Then again, if I shift the angle, other names come to mind too. No, let's save that kind of talk for the day itself, in the moment.

It's happening at Shibuya PARCO, that global stronghold of fashion.

Thank you, as always, for visiting Hobonichi today. And don't worry, the other guests will have completely different selections from mine.
