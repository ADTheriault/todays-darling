## Title: Going through today, too, with "it's no big deal."
## Date: 2026-03-06
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

There are days when I know I have to get up early the next morning. On those nights, I want to go to bed early. But I have this column, formerly known as "Today's Darling," and I can't sleep until I've finished writing the next day's piece. It would be nice if I could start writing earlier in the day, but when I start early, I end up dawdling and it takes even longer. I often think, maybe I should write a little bit during the day when I have some free time, and then just add to it later. But that's never worked out well. Actually, even when it has worked out, it hasn't made much difference. It doesn't really change anything. Usually I end up throwing away whatever I've started writing. I throw it away without hesitation and write something fresh in the middle of the night, but I can't say that produces anything particularly good either. It's been a little under 28 years now, hasn't it? I've been writing every day, so you'd think I'd have gotten a little better at it, but I haven't.

If this were a "conversation" like the one in Tasogare, which I'm serializing now, when I got to the part about getting "a little better," I'd want to interrupt myself and say, "like Akina Nakamori, you know." And then I'd want someone to come back at me with, "But Nakamori only sang it. The lyrics were written by Etsuko Kisugi." But Shinbo isn't all that familiar with this stuff, so he probably wouldn't say anything like that. He'd just laugh. Nagata has a useless wealth of knowledge about old songs too, but he probably wouldn't touch on pop music. If I really had my way, I'd go on and on about unnecessary things like "just grabbing your sleeve and looking down" or "I wonder if Akina's gotten better at it by now." Going a bit far afield, "Wait, was Iyo still 16?" "No, she was 17, right?" "Well, she's not anymore," and other pointless chatter. But look, I've already written this many words, so that's probably enough, isn't it? I can finish up now, I can go to bed early.

But I really do think, deep down, that I've been getting by all this time on being "nothing special." That I'm truly a guy who's "nothing special." Thank you for coming to Hobonichi again today. Though I wouldn't want to hear someone else say that about me. Funny, isn't it.
