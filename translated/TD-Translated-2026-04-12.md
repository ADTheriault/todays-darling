## Title: Your "amidakuji" and my "amidakuji."
## Date: 2026-04-12
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Every now and then, someone will ask me, out of the blue, "How did you first meet so-and-so?" Not in an interview or anything like that, just a crew member nearby, making conversation. Sometimes I think about it and the answer comes right away. Other times, I rack my brain and still can't figure it out. I can remember being with that person in all kinds of situations. I remember this happened, and that happened, but where did we originally meet? The very beginning, the where and when, is hazy. You could chalk it up to getting older and more forgetful, but honestly, if someone had asked me the same question ten years ago, I probably couldn't have answered it then either.

Using what I like to call my "life as a lottery grid" theory, it's like I can trace the lines just fine, but I've forgotten where the starting point was. Oh well, never mind.

This "lottery grid" does have a start, a point of origin, and that's really just the matter of where and to whom you were born. I understand that's important, of course, but after that, you turn millions of corners along the way. If I hadn't gone to that school, I never would have met that guy. If I hadn't met that guy, I never would have taken up that sport. Because I went to hang out at that place on that particular day, I met that person. If I hadn't stayed home sick from school that time, I never would have read that book. It's an endless chain of seemingly trivial coincidences and decisions, and each one changes what happens next, sending you crashing into good luck or bad.

The other day, someone said to me, "If you hadn't done that project, our children wouldn't exist." And I thought, right, because two people who met through that project ended up getting married, and those kids came into the world. That's incredible. My lottery grid overlaps and intersects with other people's grids, too.

When you think about it, even with people I've never met, you could say we've already encountered each other through some form of expression. It's almost frighteningly interesting.

My lottery grid, well, it seems like there are still plenty more corners to turn. But that's fine.

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi again today. Tracing your way back, trying to figure out where you turned each corner, that's pretty interesting too.
