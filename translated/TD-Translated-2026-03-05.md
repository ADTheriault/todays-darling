## Title: Looking at my own notes.
## Date: 2026-03-05
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

I have a note that says "a park where a child is playing first." If I organized my thoughts better, maybe I'd phrase it differently, but I haven't tinkered with it at all. I've kept that note just as it is. There's a child, playing first.

There are no words for what the child is doing. Maybe they're skipping. Maybe they're looking around at this and that. If the child's expression seems happy, someone might come join in. Even without knowing what they're doing, you can just say "let's play together" and that's enough. Or maybe you'd consult: "what should we play?" If it were puppies, there'd be no such discussion. They'd just start sniffing each other and chasing around. When one child becomes two children, the playing gets even more fun. Seeing that, another child will probably join in. And one by one, the circle of play expands. The park becomes lively, full of life.

Probably it doesn't even need to be a place called a park. Just a "space" is enough. Because one person is "playing first," there's a beginning. When there's a "space" and there's "someone playing first," there's a "future (the next moment)" right there. I guess I believe in that. I feel like that's all I've been doing.

The play doesn't even have a name yet, but the play is definitely there. The exchange of "let's play" and "play with me" enriches the play itself, enriches the space where playing happens. Spaces for play are born, they age, they disappear, but when someone starts playing there again, they're reborn.

Don't stop the play. Play is a future that keeps beginning, always.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi today. Even on days without good news, there are children playing first.
