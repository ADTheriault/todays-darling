## Title: Do you like a 50% win rate?
## Date: 2026-05-11
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

A fifty-percent win rate means no surplus and no deficit. No matter how many games you play, the wins and losses come out even. I never had a very good image of that. Especially in sports, you can't win a championship with a fifty-percent record. So whenever my favorite team was sitting at .500, I'd feel a little dejected, thinking, "They just can't pull it together."

But lately, maybe because I've grown up a bit, I've started to think, "You know, fifty percent isn't so bad."

If your wins and losses are equal, does that mean, to put it bluntly, "you might as well not have bothered"? But then I realized: that's also exactly the same as standing at the starting line, thinking, "Here we go." Having no surplus and no debt, about to begin something new. That situation is, in essence, identical to a fifty-percent win rate.

And while I'm at it, if you've played a lot of games and come out with no surplus and no deficit, it also means you've accumulated experience. It means you've been alive and moving through all that time.

To be honest, as someone who simply loves sports, I'm not satisfied with fifty percent. I can say without hesitation that I love a runaway championship victory. That's true. But I want to nurture something like a respect for that fifty-percent record.

"A .500 team you desperately want to root for." "A .500 team that feels like it could do more." "A .500 team where you can really sense the teamwork." If we could feel those things, and make others feel them too, I think there'd be so much more to enjoy.

Well, sports are a kind of stand-in for battle, so winning and losing will always matter. But in everything else we do, I think it would be wonderful if we could pull off a .500 record that has real charm.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi again today. Ambition and acceptance. Fight and peace. Passion and a gentle smile. I want them all.
