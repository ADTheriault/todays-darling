## Title: People of berets, people of wind, people of glasses.
## Date: 2026-03-04
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

People have been asking me, "Is the reason you've been wearing a beret lately because of the Hobonichi Manga Club?" It's true that manga artists like Osamu Tezuka and Fujiko Fujio A seem to favor berets. I can see why they'd think I was following in their footsteps, but that's not quite it. I've always worn berets from time to time. I used to wear all sorts of hats, not frequently, but I wore them. I got really into those Peaky Blinders style flat caps, rejecting this one and that one, ordering a bunch online. Though honestly, I hardly ever wear those. Anyway, I've always owned several berets, and one day I just thought, "Maybe I'll wear one." Then I started buying even more, wanting to choose between different ones, and before I knew it I was wearing them every day. I decided I'd stick with this until I got tired of it. (But enough about that. Let me tell you about something actually useful.)

How to avoid catching colds and keep your glasses from fogging up! Two birds with one stone, pretty amazing, right? First, for avoiding colds, nothing beats washing your hands. There's that famous question: why don't doctors catch colds even though they examine so many sick people? It's because they wash their hands frequently. So I've been trying to do the same. These days they have those pump dispensers that produce foam, which makes it incredibly easy to wash your hands often. If you don't wear glasses, you can stop reading here. But for those of us who do, listen up. When you wash your hands, wash your glasses with that same foam. While you're washing your hands anyway, use the foam on the lenses and everywhere else on the frames. Then rinse with water, not hot water because that can damage the lenses. Just shake the glasses lightly to get the water off. Any remaining drops can be absorbed with a single tissue. That's all it takes, and your glasses will be cleaner than any amount of wiping could achieve. It's so simple, I think you'll get addicted to it.

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi today. I just realized I've been writing the kind of thing you'd find in "Tasogare."
