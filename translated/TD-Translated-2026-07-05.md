## Title: On the index and ring toes, among other things.
## Date: 2026-07-05
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Every few years, I end up buying five-toe socks again. If I truly thought they were great, I'd wear them all the time. If I decided they were no good, I'd never put them on again. But somehow, after a few years pass, I find myself reaching for them once more. They're supposed to be good for you, after all. This time around, what got me started was learning a new way to put them on. Fitting each of the five toes into its little slot one by one had always been such a hassle, but the method I picked up this time makes it go relatively smoothly.

That aside, the thing that actually bothers me is what the toes are called. The big one on the inside, we call the "thumb" of the foot. And the little one on the outside is the "pinky." No argument there, no doubts whatsoever. Then the third one from the thumb, right in the middle, that's the "middle finger." Also fine. It's in the middle, so of course it's the middle finger.

But what about the one next to the thumb? Can we really call it the "pointer finger"? For a hand, sure, that makes sense. But this is a foot. Has anyone ever pointed at something with it, even once? Nobody's ever thrust it triumphantly toward the sky shouting "Number one!" either. It's a foot, after all.

And then there's one more: that hopelessly clumsy-looking fourth toe, wedged between the middle toe and the pinky. Are we really going to call that the "ring finger" with a straight face? Are you going to dab a bit of medicine on the tip of it and give it a lick? No. Nobody is doing that with that toe.

Anyway, while pondering all of this, here's the method for putting on five-toe socks. First, you pull the sock onto your "thumb," and in the same motion, slip it over the toe next to it. Then you jump straight to the unmistakable pinky. Once those three are locked in place, all doubt and confusion vanish. You just calmly fit the remaining two toes in. In numbers, it goes: 1, 2... 5, then 3, 4. Learning this technique has made the whole process so much more relaxed. Though, that said, I'll probably get bored and quit wearing them again before long.
