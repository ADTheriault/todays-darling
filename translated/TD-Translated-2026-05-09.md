## Title: Hoping for hope.
## Date: 2026-05-09
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Most people, I think, want hope. Though probably very few would put it into those exact words, openly wishing for it: "I want hope." To be honest, I myself had never said such a thing. I simply noticed that somewhere deep down, the feeling was there.

Every day, a relentless stream of news reaches us. So much of it is bad. And most of it is probably true, so you take it in as best you can. What to do about any of it is never easy to figure out. Ideas and projections pour in too, about the state of things now, about what might be coming next. You absorb them, but it's hard to imagine much of it leading anywhere good.

Wait, I thought. I can't see any hope at all in any of this. That was the moment it hit me. Among everything being reported, everything I was learning, everything I was being made to think about, there was so remarkably little that pointed toward hope.

Well then, was there more hope in the past? Hmm, I suppose hope has always been hard to find. But when I saw, say, a middle school kid throwing everything they had into something, or anyone who seemed to carry hope inside them, there it was. Hope was right there.

Maybe that's the thing. Maybe people who have hope are themselves the hope.

You can try all you want to make someone understand just how bleak the world is. A person who carries hope will go right on carrying it. Sure, there are con artists who wave hope around like a banner. And people who crush hope, people who mock it. Of course there are. But even knowing all that, hope doesn't just disappear. There are no grounds for hope, no guarantees. A baby's very first cry is hope itself.

I've decided, all over again, to be someone who looks for hope.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi again today. A child running full speed through the park. A person whose face breaks into a smile when they say good morning.
