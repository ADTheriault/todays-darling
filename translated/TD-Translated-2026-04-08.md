## Title: Thinking in progress: life is a series of things left unfinished.
## Date: 2026-04-08
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

How many things do I have going half-finished, I wonder. Not pushing myself too hard, just muddling along with all these things left midway. It's always been like this. It's practically a habit of mine. Even so, there really are a lot of them.

And it's not just work-related things. Books I want to read keep piling up near where I sit. The "invisible hand" of our household seems to gather them up and relocate them elsewhere, but then the newly cleared space just fills up with books again. And streaming shows: honestly, I want to watch all of them. But the ones stuck on "continue watching" and the ones sitting in "my list" just keep accumulating.

Fortunately, when it comes to events, there's the hassle of having to make reservations, which means that unless something really comes up, I actually go. In that sense, there's no "half-finished" problem there. As for wanting to eat certain things, well, aside from the places that absolutely require reservations, when it comes to ramen, for instance, I just keep stockpiling information. I love the show "Eating Ramen" on BS-TBS.

When it comes to seeing people, now that I've gotten older, I try to remind myself, "I might never get to see them again." And this is a terrible thing to say, but it applies to the other person too. I've decided to think, as much as I can, "They might not be able to see me anymore, either." But I can't exactly meet everyone whenever I want, so this, too, just keeps piling up.

And yet, when I really think about it, there's no such thing as having everything wrap up neatly right when you die. From the moment we're born until the moment we die, of course we're always in the middle of something. That's just how it is.

Having a mountain of half-finished things. Does that make me greedy? Does it mean I'm living with everything I've got? Either way, it's not a bad thing at all. Even if it is completely exhausting.

Thank you, as always, for coming to Hobonichi today. Though I notice I ended on "completely exhausting." Ha.
