## Title: Fried rice is egg on rice.
## Date: 2026-07-03
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Lately, I've been eating a lot of fried rice at old-school Chinese joints around town. It's not so much that I love fried rice or have a craving for it. It's more that I find myself genuinely impressed, all over again, by how its popularity never seems to fade. I've developed a serious interest. Like going to see a veteran singer who's been delivering great performances for years, I order the fried rice and savor it from every angle.

While I sit there eating my fried rice with this slightly sideways fascination, two more customers who came in after me have already ordered, without a moment's hesitation, "Fried rice!" One after the other.

I'd always assumed fried rice was something you ordered as a finishing carb at the end of a meal. But it turns out a meal of nothing but fried rice is perfectly complete. After all, it usually comes with a small bowl of soup. There's pickled red ginger, or zha cai, or some kind of pickle served alongside. It varies from place to place, but the main protein is usually roast pork. And it goes without saying that green onion is the ingredient doing all the behind-the-scenes work, pulling together the aroma, the flavor, and the texture to make that pork shine. If there's no green onion, I'd rather skip the fried rice that day. Bits of naruto fish cake? You can leave them out, and honestly it's better without. But green onion is absolutely essential. Borrow some from the restaurant next door if you have to.

So what is fried rice, at its core? It's rice. And really, it's egg-on-rice. Fried rice is stir-fried egg-on-rice. You scatter green onion over it as a seasoning and mix in some roast pork. Egg-on-rice is already a meal that stands on its own. When all else fails, egg-on-rice will get you through. How much more so, then, with fried rice. The reason fried rice never loses its popularity is that, at heart, it's egg-on-rice.

Incidentally, I have invented something I call "ramen fried rice." You stir-fry rice with egg, then add roast pork, chopped menma bamboo shoots, and green onion. Season it with the sauce from the roast pork, and you'd do well to add some pepper too. That's it.

Thank you, as always, for visiting Hobonichi today. If anyone knows a neighborhood Chinese place that puts menma in their fried rice, I'd love to go.
