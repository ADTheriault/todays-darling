## Title: In just 20 years, this little box has.
## Date: 2026-04-03
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

There was a time when, if you were heading off to some southern island for a long vacation, your luggage would be absurdly heavy. You were obviously going to want to read while you were there, so you had to choose your books. Even trying to keep it minimal, you'd end up with at least ten. Then there was music: you'd pack cassette tape after cassette tape. That alone took up a surprising amount of space. Later, even when CDs came along, the dilemma persisted. And of course, you needed a device to play the music on. Whether it was cassettes or CDs, you needed batteries, so you'd think, better bring some of those too. Just the things I've listed so far would fill a large trunk. You'd stuff swimwear and sandals and a few changes of clothes into the gaps, and haul the whole groaning load to the airport. Then, for a while after that, a heavy, bulky "computer" got added to the mix. Just reading this far is exhausting, isn't it? Writing it, I'm amazed I actually used to do all that.

But here's the thing: all of that exhausting stuff, minus the swim trunks and sandals, now fits neatly inside one tiny little box. That entire mountain of luggage fits right in your pocket. That's right, the thing they call a "smartphone." And forget ten or twenty books, that's thinking small. You're carrying an entire library of tens of thousands of volumes. As for music, you could honestly say that just about every song anyone in the world has ever heard of is in there. Go ahead, throw in a "camera" and a "wallet" too. And, would you believe it, there's even a "movie theater" in there. No need to stock up on batteries, either.

And listen, this "smartphone" thing? You can make phone calls to anywhere in the world with it. If you want to write a letter, you can do that too, and send it instantly. The person on the other end can read it right away. I used to daydream about having some kind of magical tool like this. And the smartphone has surpassed even those daydreams. Of course everyone wants one. Of course everyone's obsessed. And all of this happened in just twenty years. Incredible.

Thank you, as always, for coming to Hobonichi today. You know how infants go through weaning? I'm starting to think I need to go through something like that with my smartphone.
