## Title: Perhaps the nights are longer than the days.
## Date: 2026-05-01
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

People sometimes compare a human life to the movement of the sun. It's actually something most of us do, in one way or another.

The time just after birth is like the sun rising from the horizon. A baby lights up everything around it, and just watching one fills you with joy. Before long, the sun climbs higher and higher. The light grows stronger, and perhaps the sun itself is growing larger. It feels like an afternoon that will never end. Sometimes the glare is so fierce it withers the grass and trees. In the blazing season of youth, you need the help of water and wind.

But of course, the afternoon cannot last forever. At some point, the sun is no longer at its highest. Now it descends toward the earth. The brightness fades, and the glare is gone. In the western sky, a soft, faintly reddish light sinks quietly away.

You might think that's the end of the sun's story, but it isn't quite. And if you're wondering whether the moon and stars step in to take the sun's place, well, it doesn't really work out that neatly. Nor is this where a person's life simply ends. What happens, more or less, is that night begins. And it is long. Every bit as long as the day. Long, long, long. You could sleep through it if you wanted, or you could stay awake the way you did during the daytime.

And if you sit there wondering what to do with this long night, eventually, from the eastern sky, here it comes again. The sun. Well now, is that how it works? It just repeats? Even if you compare a single day to a human life, wait a moment, there's no ending to this, is there?

When I think back, I parted ways with friends I used to play with. I grew tired of things, forgot things, one after another. But even after nightfall, there are still people to meet, and the music keeps playing, calling out, "Come on, let's have fun." After the sunset, the night turns out to be lit up bright as anything.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi today. I'm just singing whatever comes to mind, the way you might hum a lullaby for someone.
