## Title: Everyone has more energy than they know what to do with.
## Date: 2026-05-19
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

It's possible that a great many people are thinking something like this: "If someone would just tell me clearly, 'Do this,' if someone would just give me specific instructions on what I'm supposed to do, I could really show what I'm capable of."

They might also be thinking, "If someone would just present me with a goal, a bright future to work toward, I'd give it everything I've got. I'd work myself to the bone."

And I suspect that's probably true. I think there are an enormous number of people who believe, "The reason I can't perform at my full potential is that nobody's given me something I should be doing, and I haven't found anything I want to do."

In dramas, there's often a leader you can look up to, and the story revolves around the team rallying together to help him succeed. You see it in sports stories, war stories, corporate sagas. The team members in those stories already know what needs to be done. So no matter how hard it gets, no matter how impossible it seems, no matter what sacrifices are required, all they have to do is throw themselves into it with everything they've got.

I imagine some people can't help saying things like, "If I were in that kind of situation, I could get serious too," or "It's because the people at the top don't have a clear vision." I get it. It feels like you've got all this untapped energy just waiting to be unleashed.

But here's the thing. Situations where you know exactly what needs to be done and all that's left is to give it your all, those situations are becoming rarer and rarer in this day and age, whatever it might have been like in the past.

And if what needs to be done is already clear, isn't that exactly the kind of thing AI will end up handling instead of humans?

You have the ladder. You have the strength to climb it. But you don't know where to lean it or where it's supposed to take you. In times like these, actually having something you want to do, something you feel you should do, that's an incredible gift. Maybe creativity is precisely this: making the situation where you can bring your strength to bear.

Thanks for coming to Hobonichi again today. First, ask yourself what you want. Even if it's hazy, just try looking at it.
