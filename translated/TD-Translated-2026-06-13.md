## Title: I took notes outside the movie theater.
## Date: 2026-06-13
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

You know how raindrops, falling one by one onto a stone, can eventually wear a hole right through it? It really does happen. And even the tiniest droplet of a thought, if you keep thinking it over and over, might eventually become a word, like a hollow carved into stone.

Yesterday I went to see the Spanish film *Cerrar los ojos*, and, well, I'll try not to say much about the content, but it was extraordinary. The images just kept washing over me, one after another. The screenplay, the music and sound design, the direction, the acting, the cinematography, the world they built around it all. Every element felt flawless. You might even call it a perfectly played straight face, so committed there's nothing to challenge. It could be read as a grand tragicomedy, or something supremely cerebral, or something with a realism so vivid you'd mistake it for a documentary.

Starting from the point of "this is the film I want to make" and arriving at "it's finished!" in reality. The sheer toughness it must have taken, mentally, physically, financially, temporally, practically knocked me flat.

Seeing this incredible film must have converged with something I'd been feeling for a long time, like those steady, patient raindrops. The word "LIFE," and all the goodness in it, rose up again, towering before me.

I don't want to state these as arguments or positions. I want to write them as short, poem-like words, something I can set down right in front of me.

Don't cut corners on things. Cutting corners is cutting corners on your life.

A single day's living is a miniature of a whole life's living. What you seek today is what you seek for your entire life.

Cheap, fast, and in bulk? If you want to live a cheap, fast, bulk-produced life, be my guest.

I was scribbling notes like these as I walked out of the theater. The area around Shibuya Station was packed with countless lives, all jostling together. Everyone, living however they please. And that's fine.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi again today. LIFE: it means life, living, livelihood, the raw and immediate, the whole of one's days. It matters.
