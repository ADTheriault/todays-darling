## Title: Writing is hard enough as it is.
## Date: 2026-05-29
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

No, wait, wait, wait. I just realized something incredible. We live our lives using Japanese, but when someone actually tells us to sit down and write something, more often than not we struggle with how hard it is. (Don't we?) If someone said, "Write about anything you want, around a thousand characters," what would you write, and how? You might think a hundred characters would be easier, but that's hard in its own way. Ten thousand characters? That's enormously, enormously difficult. A thousand, a hundred, a hundred thousand, or even just twenty: writing is hard no matter the length. And when you think about it, this has always been taken for granted. That's exactly why people who possessed the skill of putting things into words were called "good writers" or "authors" or "professionals," and could make a living from it.

From elementary school kids to full-grown adults to seasoned veterans of life, writing has always been a difficult thing. On top of that, writing something grammatically correct, logically consistent, free of typos and missing characters, and interesting on top of all that? I'd say eighty percent of the population would proudly declare, "Can't do it." I'd like to humbly include myself in that eighty percent, despite having spent all these years disguised as a professional and making a career of it.

I write on Hobonichi every single day. Today, same as always, I'm sitting here writing away like it's nothing. But I'm sure that anyone who actually specializes in writing would be furious at the sloppiness and haphazardness of what I keep putting out there for all to see. After all these years of writing, this is where I am. Am I an idiot?

So yes, writing has always been supposed to be hard. And yet AI can do it effortlessly. Without any practice at all, whether it's a thousand characters, a hundred thousand, a hundred, or twenty, it just keeps producing text. Just now, I tried asking it to "write something, anything," changing the specified length each time, running the experiment over and over. Long or short, every piece came out coherent, grammatically correct, and reasonably interesting, all in a matter of seconds.

How is it that this was so hard for us all along?
