## Title: Soaking in a darkened bath with my eyes closed.
## Date: 2026-06-02
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Whether you sleep well or not is beside the point. Just closing your eyes and lying down is enough, apparently. I've heard that plenty of times myself, but when someone else says it to you again, it brings the idea back fresh.

The "Life Enjoyment Market" got off to a really wonderful start. Everyone was busy in their own way, but all in all it was a calm, gentle day. Of course there were things that needed more thought, things we didn't handle well. That's inevitable, and I want to embrace all of it, the rough parts included. Still, the lack of sleep is something I know I'm not alone in, and that's a separate matter worth thinking about.

I soaked in a lukewarm bath, closed my eyes, and lay back. The bathroom light was off. It really does feel good. I wasn't asleep, but it was something very close. This must be a good thing, I thought. This is what matters.

And then, of course, I couldn't help myself. I started wondering what exactly makes it feel so good.

Being defenseless, maybe. That's what I think it is. Right. Most of the time, we're on guard. We do it because we have to, but it must put us under a tremendous amount of tension. So you create a moment where you can let that go, and you become completely unguarded. It really is quite similar to sleeping soundly, in total safety.

We human adults love to watch things that are completely, utterly defenseless. A baby, or a young animal, sleeping in the most perfect peace imaginable. When I think that we live in the same world as that seemingly infinite sense of safety, it makes me happy. All the worry, the anxiety, the suspicion, the combativeness, all of it fades away, and you feel like following those fragile little beings who've already gone ahead into that unguarded place.

That. That's what I need, properly, every single day. I have to take in that defenselessness like nourishment. Even just closing your eyes in the bathtub is a small step in the right direction.

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi again today. I'm writing this after getting out of the bath. Ooh, I'm sleepy.
