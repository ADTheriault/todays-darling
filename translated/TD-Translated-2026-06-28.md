## Title: It's Saturday, but I went to Kanda for tonkatsu.
## Date: 2026-06-28
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

When the tonkatsu arrives at the table, right there in front of me, it always begins with the same thought: it's still so hot. I separate three slices from the left side of the cutlet, pulling them slightly away from the rest. A light squeeze of lemon, just a whisper of it, then soy sauce dripped on carefully so as not to use too much. The hottest, freshest pieces I eat this way. Oh, and a dab of mustard too, I almost forgot. My front teeth bite through the crust, and it shatters gently, like a thin sheet of ice. Then the meat, the pork, is instantly converted into pure deliciousness and spreads across my mouth. The aroma, the flavor, the perfect tenderness, all of it touching everywhere, the top of my tongue, the insides of my cheeks, and the story of the tonkatsu begins to unfold. I chew with my back teeth, savoring again, jaw and tongue moving as desire commands. More chewing, swallowing, biting down harder. When I really press into it, I can feel the rich fat seeping out.

I deliberately avoided inspecting the cross-section too closely, but the first two slices or so from the left side are the fattiest part, and that fat is precisely what makes the "special loin" worth boasting about. While my tongue registers the lush, spreading richness of the fat, I take a bite of rice. The harmony of tonkatsu fat and plain white rice plays like music inside my mouth.

Tonkatsu sauce comes after that. Here, too, I'm careful not to overdo it, applying the sauce one slice at a time, adding mustard, savoring the texture of the crust against my teeth. By this point I've settled in, and the pace of my chewing slows. I lift my gaze slightly and think: this is so good, so good again today. When I'm eating at the counter and happen to catch the eye of the owner who does the frying, I give a small nod. The cabbage, the miso soup, the pickled daikon and lightly salted pickles, the rice. I give thanks to all the supporting players that make the tonkatsu taste even better.

When the plate is empty, it's time for the check. This is a place I know well, so I say what I feel, simply and sincerely. "That was truly delicious." I say it twice, and bow.

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi again today. I'd already decided on Friday that Saturday would be a tonkatsu day.
