## Title: People I admire, who are incredibly resilient.
## Date: 2026-05-17
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

There are things that sound completely blunt and obvious, yet are fundamentally, incredibly important. For example, something I've written about here a few times: when Takaaki Yoshimoto spoke about why he became interested in Shinran, he said it was largely because he grew up in a household deeply devoted to Jodo Shinshu Buddhism. Or when someone asked him, "Why do you always have the TV on?" and he answered, "Probably because I get lonely." These aren't the kind of impressive-sounding answers people expect, so an interviewer might feel let down. But they get at something truly fundamental. And what I want to say is very much in that same vein.

I saw Kenji Ozawa's incredibly powerful live show, and all I can talk about is the sheer energy of it. I understand what he's going for, what he wants people to feel. But if you think of it in terms of a tree, all of that belongs to the flowers and the fruit. And I'm all for everyone admiring and discussing how magnificent those are. But what I kept sensing was the root force, the fundamental power, root and trunk, that makes those flowers and fruit possible. That's all I wanted to talk about. Bluntly, with no regard for politeness or decorum, what I thought was: "That guy is ridiculously tough!"

He looks like he might be some delicate, sensitive young gentleman, but really he's storing up an almost ferocious amount of energy. It reminds me, I think Seiji Ozawa must have been the same way, just brimming with energy. All the truly extraordinary people in the world have incredible physical stamina. I bet that's true of politicians too, isn't it? And in cases where someone doesn't have that natural stamina, they grind themselves down to nothing, pour out every last ounce of abnormal energy, and essentially burn through their whole life doing it.

Yesterday I went to see Reiwa Roman's big live show, and the energy they put behind every laugh was like a weapon going off. People who can do that kind of thing are the ones who draw others to them. And me? I'm frail, low-energy, and honestly kind of pathetic.

Thanks for coming to Hobonichi again today. I'd like to find a way to keep things interesting, even if I stay pathetic.
