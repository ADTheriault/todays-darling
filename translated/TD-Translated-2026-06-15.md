## Title: Sentences that you don't need to read or write.
## Date: 2026-06-15
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

It's Sunday evening, and here I am, sitting at a loss. "Here" being my work desk, the place where I write. I haven't even decided what to write about, yet here I am, typing away. The reason is simple: I don't want to be doing this work late at night, as I usually do. My ambition tonight is to be in bed by ten. Because I want to be awake by four, four-thirty, the hour I'd normally be crawling into bed. And from there, I want to keep my eyes open for about three hours straight. On top of that, Monday's schedule has three things I need to get done. So I really don't want to spend the day sleep-deprived.

Write tomorrow's column during the daytime on Sunday. Then eat dinner, take a bath, and get to bed early. That's the unfamiliar plan I'm attempting here.

As you've probably guessed, it's for the soccer. The World Cup, Japan versus the Netherlands. I intend to watch it believing we'll win, but of course winning isn't guaranteed. I'm fairly well acquainted with the dejection that comes from a loss. Even if I manage to pull off this forced early-to-bed, early-to-rise routine, the exhaustion when they don't win is going to be brutal.

As you may be aware, yours truly is an aging body running low on stamina. And yet I will push myself to watch and cheer through the entire broadcast. No, forgive me if that sounds like I'm fishing for sympathy. What I'm doing is perfectly ordinary. Just the story of one unremarkable day. Though it's also true that it wears me out.

I should just rest. I could record the game and watch it later at my leisure. I know that. It's not as though I haven't had that argument with myself. But I'm a crew member of the "Put Hands and Feet on Your Dreams" team. If you want to watch it, then what can you do right now? If you don't want it interfering with tomorrow's work, what should you do right now? Write the column on Sunday evening. That's the answer.

Fortunately, I already watched "The Toyotomi Brothers" on BS-4K just after noon. The daytime Seibu-Giants game ended quietly. (From here on out, just as before, the world keeps moving, and so do I...)

By the way, by the time you're reading this, how did the match turn out?

Thank you, as always, for coming to Hobonichi today. To rid myself of any second thoughts, I'm going to go ahead and send this piece off right now.
