## Title: There isn't that much I can answer
## Date: 2026-06-06
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

When I was a child, when I was young, adults would ask me, "What do you want to be when you grow up?" and the truth was, I had no idea. When my friends talked about which singers or actors on TV or in the movies they liked best, I didn't know that either. I felt like I had to pick something, had to give an answer. The truth was I never really knew, but I'd think it over and somehow settle on something. It was always a forced decision. The fact is, not every question has a ready answer. That's true for children, and it's just as true for adults.

You can't have a reaction to everything, put every single thing into words. It's not a matter of failing to "verbalize" your feelings. Half the time, there's simply no reaction there to begin with. If someone told me I was lacking in sensitivity, well, maybe so. If someone asked whether I was feeling anything at all, the honest answer would be: maybe not, maybe so.

There are ways of feeling that you don't even notice yourself, and sometimes it takes years, a very long time, before you finally understand what they were. This happens all the time with relationships. The people closest to you, your father, your mother. What I actually felt about them, what I actually thought, I don't think I ever really understood. Love, dislike, gratitude, resentment. Honestly, I couldn't sort out any of those feelings.

People can't just answer whatever they're asked. Does anyone actually exist who has an answer for everything? You can't figure it out. You can't figure out other people, and you can't figure out yourself. And on top of all that, gradually feeling things, gradually coming to understand, that's what it means to be alive. That's enough to hold on to.

People toss out difficult questions so carelessly, so easily, as though they were nothing.

Thanks for coming to Hobonichi today. Yesterday, too, there was so much I didn't understand. And you know what? It was a good day.
