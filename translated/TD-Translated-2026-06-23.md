## Title: It's only after your ignorance is exposed that the real challenge begins.
## Date: 2026-06-23
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Over the course of a lifetime, we come into contact with an enormous number of people. We do things together, we observe each other. So many people share the same time, the same spaces with us. Family, friends, people we play with or work alongside, the staff at places we frequent, our customers.

Some we've known since childhood. Others we met during those brash, restless years of adolescence or early adulthood. There are people we fell in love with, and people in front of whom we embarrassed ourselves.

No matter how much you try to make yourself look impressive, no matter how much you dress things up, no matter how wise or kind you try to appear, the people who've been around you for a long time pretty much see through it. Your failings, your flaws, your selfishness, your dishonesty, your weaknesses. They end up knowing nearly all of it. When people have been watching you up close for years, there's simply nowhere to hide. Even those who say the most admirable things are often quite different at home.

When you've met enough people, spent time with enough people, done enough things, talked enough, eventually people come to understand you so thoroughly that you start to think, "Well, there's probably nothing left they haven't figured out." In other words, if you live long enough, people have long since figured out that you're a fool.

As you've probably guessed, I'm talking about myself here. Especially after all these years of sitting down day after day, groaning my way through even these short pieces of writing. No matter how I try to put on a good face, it's perfectly clear by now that I'm nothing all that special.

I'm not sure exactly when I realized this. But at some point, without quite noticing, I gave something up, and I arrived at this way of thinking: life really begins after everyone's figured out you're a fool.

Of course, it's not as though I've lost all vanity or desire. But I do feel I've made a fair amount of peace with it. And that, I think, makes it easier to be free. You know?

Thanks for coming to Hobonichi again today. Well, I suppose I've spent most of this long journey in my everyday clothes anyway.
