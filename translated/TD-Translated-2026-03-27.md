## Title: Just thinking about it isn't a crime.
## Date: 2026-03-27
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

It might be too grand to call it a thought experiment, but every now and then I find myself turning over things I've never really considered before. For instance, something like this.

I say this in all seriousness: year after year, I've been working harder and getting busier. Back in my thirties, I suppose, there was a period when I must have looked like the very picture of a man in demand. And at the time, I certainly felt busy. The volume of work I was handling then was, by any measure, considerable. But looking back now, if I'd been the person I later became, I could have managed that workload far more easily.

This isn't a boast. I can say it precisely because it's about myself. The truth is, back then, an enormous amount of time was spent "thinking I was thinking," when really I was doing nothing at all. I dressed up huge stretches of time as a running start toward work, while actually I was out having fun or enjoying other things. I'd stay up all hours of the night, doing very little actual work.

Still, what I can see clearly now is this: the fundamental abilities I have as a person today were built during that era, in all that wasted time. The habits and methods I rely on when coming up with ideas, I believe I picked those up during the years when I wasn't really working at all.

But after that, especially after Hobonichi, more and more of my time got allocated toward things that were useful. Truly, every hour became a running start toward some piece of work, or study aimed at making work go better. I was always asking myself, "Isn't there more I could be doing?" I became my own taskmaster, driving myself to stay busy.

And here, abruptly, is where that "thought experiment" I mentioned at the start comes in. What if, say, I cut twenty percent of the things I consider "my work"? Wouldn't my productivity actually go up by twenty percent? "Doing less work raises productivity." Is that too much of a paradox? And yet, in this day and age, it's something I genuinely want to think through.

Thank you, as always, for visiting Hobonichi today. Does time spent working equal earnings produced? In the age of AI, that equation is going to change, isn't it.
