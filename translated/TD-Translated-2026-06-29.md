## Title: Looking at a teddy bear, lost in thought.
## Date: 2026-06-29
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

"If you hate the monk, you'll hate his robe too." It's a very well-known proverb. The idea is that once you've decided you dislike a monk, even the robe he wears starts to seem hateful. It's a quirk of human psychology. When you think of it as being literally about monks and robes, it might not feel especially relevant to your own life, but really, most of us do this kind of thing all the time. You hate someone, so you hate their entire profession. You can't stand a certain person, so you can't stand the things they like. You dislike a team, so you despise their mascot. We slip into these feelings without even thinking about it. (And of course, it works in reverse too: you love someone, and suddenly their favorite food becomes your favorite food.)

Still, I think what matters is being aware of this tendency, this habitual quirk of human psychology, and learning to keep it in check. That's what I believe, anyway. But the world doesn't work that neatly. Once you've labeled someone the "monk" (i.e., the enemy), the people around them start to look like enemies too, and everything associated with them gets treated as the "robe" (i.e., the enemy's weapon). Whatever the "monk" says must be a lie, all of it. Human history has been doing this sort of thing forever. Maybe it was even worse in the past than it is now. Then again, things have been pretty rough in the age of the internet, too.

But then, you don't hear about anyone who's afraid of bears, who hates bears, saying they also hate teddy bears or Winnie-the-Pooh. I've never met anyone who's furious at Iran and has therefore developed a grudge against Persian rugs. Outside of a certain handful of, shall we say, very intense people, most of us are actually fairly level-headed about these things.

For what it's worth, I'm allergic to shrimp and crab, but I don't hate them and I'm not angry at them. If I'm reborn in another life, I want to eat all the shrimp and crab I can get.
