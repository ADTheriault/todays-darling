## Title: To those of us who had no plans for the long holiday.
## Date: 2026-04-22
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

By this point in April, even I start to feel the pressure. Golden Week comes for everyone, even those of us who aren't the type to make plans. The organized people have already figured out what they're doing and locked in their schedules, but then there are people who haven't. People like me, for instance. I just don't have the resolve to go through the trouble of making reservations, packing for a trip, juggling schedules, and throwing myself into the crowds. I'll just drift along, day by day, depending on the weather. Once I decided that, it was honestly a relief.

And yet, this year's holiday stretch is supposed to be a good one. Because "Hobonichi Sunday," over at Shibuya PARCO, is turning into the "kayōkyoku café" I've always dreamed of.

There used to be jazz cafés, and rock cafés. Places where you'd drink tea and listen to music on a great sound system. I used to go to places like that all the time. But was there ever a kayōkyoku café? Probably not, because kayōkyoku was just "regular music." It was everywhere already, at home, on the street, so there was no need to go to a special place to hear it.

But that world doesn't exist anymore. Songs have become something you listen to as an individual, wherever you happen to be. I understand the appeal of that, and I'm grateful for it. But feeling the waves of sound travel through a room and wash over your whole body, or listening in a place where other people are listening too, that's a deeply happy thing.

And kayōkyoku, in the broad sense that includes pop, the hit songs of the day, is just so good. The melodies, the lyrics, the music. As for what exactly makes it all so good, the evening's guest will surely tell you, with a whole lot of love.

I'll be taking a turn as one of those guests myself, but really, I plan to be in the audience almost every day, just another regular at the café. (That's the whole point, right? A café you keep coming back to.) No big applause, no cheering. Just good music played on good speakers. And every night, it'll be one of those events where we say, "We're doing this even if nobody shows up."

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi today. It's like hanging around the classroom after school, everyone just sort of gathering to listen to music together.
