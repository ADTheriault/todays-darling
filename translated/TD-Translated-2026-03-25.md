## Title: Sharing disappointment with someone.
## Date: 2026-03-25
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Yesterday, I saw Hiroyuki Ariyoshi post on Twitter (X): "The zoo was closed. I was so sad I almost cried." Reading that, I couldn't help writing back: "This brought back memories of times like that. When you share a disappointment with your kid, it becomes a good memory in its own way." And it really is true.

I wasn't a particularly great father, but I did take my daughter all sorts of places. It was a different era, before the internet, and there wasn't this culture of booking reservations for every little thing. So we'd head out all excited only to find the place was closed. Or it would be so packed we'd just turn around and go home. If we went fishing, we'd catch absolutely nothing. Once, on a trip, my daughter came down with a fever. All kinds of things happened.

But the fact that we tasted those disappointments together, that's something I can look back on now and feel genuinely glad about. Serious injuries or accidents, those are a different story. But everything short of that, the little failures and bits of bad luck, each one has become a fond memory. Laughing together is wonderful, being moved together is wonderful, but being let down together, even crying together, those are truly precious as memories between "you and me."

"Sharing a disappointment together." It was a phrase that came to me on the spot, thinking about parents and children. But it applies just as well to partners, friends, colleagues. Those shared memories turn out to be so important. Of course, nobody goes looking for disappointments or failures. But they're not exactly something you can avoid, either. Joy, anger, sorrow, happiness, triumph, defeat: more or less all of it comes around eventually. Having someone you can look back with and say, "Man, that was rough, wasn't it?" is, when you think about it later, a real blessing.

Oh, wait. Maybe what makes us happiest, in the end, is the experiences and the relationships that stay in our hearts. I only just thought of this, so I can't quite put it into words yet.

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi today. What holds everything up is eating well, sleeping well, and playing well.
