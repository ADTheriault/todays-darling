## Title: Failure and chance are the ingredients for hope.
## Date: 2026-03-13
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

I was raised on the principle of "better safe than sorry, and then safer still." I think parents and teachers, regardless of generation, all said something along these lines: "No matter how sure you feel, you never know what might happen. If you consider every possibility and prepare for it, you'll have nothing to worry about, no matter what comes your way." Growing up steeped in that kind of teaching, I think I ended up narrowing my own possibilities considerably.

"Consider every possibility and prepare for it" is certainly one way to avoid failure. In medicine, in rail and aviation, in accounting, it's an essential way of thinking, and genuinely important. But if everyone applies this approach to every situation in life, it only ratchets up the tension, clouds your judgment, and blurs the focus on what truly matters.

It may be late in the game, but what I want to say (to myself as much as anyone) is this: "good judgment" is not the same thing as "considering every possibility" or "being extra, extra careful." (Besides, in this day and age, AI is better at that sort of thing anyway.) What matters far more is never losing sight of what's important, being willing to make a decision even when things aren't perfect, and not putting things off too long. If parents and teachers are going to teach anything, I think it should be things like that.

In the real world, where real people are living and moving, trying a lot of things yields far greater rewards. In that world, failures and chance encounters are precious raw material. Rather than being so cautious you never fail, rather than having a neat logical explanation for everything, the encounters you gain through action, including the failures and the accidents, are worth so much more. I really do think so. Hey, I'm talking to you, me.

The self that was shaped by the adults of the past and the self I am now go back and forth, making our way toward whatever comes next.
