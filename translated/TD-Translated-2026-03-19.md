## Title: On fairies and such.
## Date: 2026-03-19
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Part One: "On Fairies."

There are people on this earth I think of as "fairies." Not the kind with wings on their backs, hovering like hummingbirds and sipping nectar from flowers. They look like ordinary humans, living human lives in human society. They're not necessarily delicate young girls, either. Quite often they're old ladies, or middle-aged men.

At first glance, there's nothing fairy-like about them at all. But when you say, "You know that person? Don't you think they're a fairy?" the people around you will often nod and say, "Oh. Yes, I see it."

I'll say it again: no wings, and entirely human in appearance. Yet they are deeply good-natured, in a way that includes a touch of mischief. Somewhere in their minds they carry a patch of perfectly clear blue sky, and every now and then their hearts just start to dance. That's the species I'm talking about.

They stretch twenty-four hours into something more like thirty-six. Without telling a soul, they slip off to play hide-and-seek all by themselves, leaving the rest of us humans stranded here on the ground. I'm one of those humans, so it's only later that I realize, "Ah, the fairy has flown off into the sky." Maybe they really did fly away, wings or not. Maybe they'll come tumbling back with some strange little shout. That's just what fairies do. There's nothing for it.

Part Two: "And So On."

You go out to eat somewhere, and there are places that make you think, "I want to come back here." Then there's the opposite: it was every bit as good as people said, but somehow you feel, "I don't think I'll be back." Nothing was wrong with it. The food was delicious. It's just that if you ask yourself whether you'd go again, the answer is probably not.

It's not only restaurants. The same thing happens with people. There are those you think, "I'll see them again. I want to." Even while you're still together, you already feel sure you'll meet again. And then there are others, nothing wrong with them at all, where you quietly sense, "I probably won't see this person again."

I can never quite pin down what makes the difference. But I've decided it's probably fine not to know.

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi again today. I wrote this one like a diary, without really thinking about whoever might read it.
