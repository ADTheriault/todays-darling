## Title: I'm on margarine's side.
## Date: 2026-04-16
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

First, let me state something that should be taken as a given: butter is delicious. Butter is delicious, and I love butter. At some point, I started preferring buttercream cakes even over fresh cream ones. The buttercream of the old days wasn't very good, but today's buttercream, made with real confidence, is wonderful. It's made of butter, so of course it's wonderful.

With that established, I want to come to the defense of margarine. Margarine has been looked down on for a long time. There was a brief period when people said that plant-based margarine was healthier than the milk fat in butter, and margarine enjoyed a little moment in the sun. But then, at some point, it came under tremendous fire for containing high levels of trans fats. Since then, manufacturing methods have actually been improved, and apparently the problem has been resolved. Still, the vague notion that "margarine is bad for you, isn't it?" seems to persist.

When I learned all this, I decided I'd give margarine a little encouragement by switching to it for my bread. It spreads easily on toast and sandwiches, which is nice. And it tastes good in its own right. Hang in there, margarine. That was how I felt.

Margarine can do an impression of butter, but butter can't do an impression of margarine. And it spreads so much more easily. Butter isn't bad, butter is actually great, but margarine hasn't done anything to deserve being disliked.

That's how I think about it as I eat my daily toast. I'm rooting for margarine while also, sure, eating butter too. I'm not the enemy of either one. I don't want to cause a schism in the world of fats and oils. My taste preference leans slightly toward butter, but I use more margarine by volume. It's just a feeling, but this seems very like me.

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi again today. You could call it a lack of straightforwardness, or you could call it a desire for balance.
