## Title: Eating biang biang noodles.
## Date: 2026-04-14
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

I'm no particular foodie, and I wouldn't say I'm exceptionally lonely, but I do fairly often go out to eat something on my own. Sometimes far away, sometimes nearby. On Saturdays or Sundays, if it's within an hour by train, I'll make the trip. Well, let's say it happens from time to time. And almost always, it's for ramen. I can't hold a candle to the true ramen devotees out there, but I do watch the BS-TBS show "Eating Ramen" regularly.

When I've traveled a long way, the food already tastes good before I've even started eating. I went there to enjoy it, so of course it's going to be good. If the same shop were right in my neighborhood, maybe it wouldn't hit the same way, but having made the long trip out, it tastes better by exactly that much. "Eating" begins with "going there to eat." And before "going," I think there's "imagining."

Following that line of thinking, I had wanted to eat biang biang noodles for a very long time. There is actually a Chinese character for the "biang biang" part, but it's so complicated that the font can't even render it here. The total stroke count is supposedly somewhere between 50 and 60 (they can't even agree on that?), and apparently the character was created specifically for these noodles. The character itself comes up all the time as a bit of trivia, but I didn't know anyone nearby who had actually eaten biang biang noodles. Everyone was eager to explain the character, yet nobody could tell me what the noodles were actually like.

Then one day I learned that there was a biang biang noodle shop right in my own neighborhood, in Kanda Jimbocho of all places. But precisely because it was so close, I figured I could go anytime, and I just left it at that, thinking I'd get around to it eventually. Then the other day, purely by chance, I happened to walk right past the place and thought, "So this is it!" Fortune favors those who wander. I went yesterday!

What were the noodles like? Were they thick? Were they not thin? I'm not telling. I walked in, looked at the menu, and asked, "Which one is the biang biang noodles?" The answer was, "All of them." And that's how it began.

I won't teach you the character, and I won't tell you about the noodles. Even if I leave it at that, it's not going to cause any trouble in your life, is it?
