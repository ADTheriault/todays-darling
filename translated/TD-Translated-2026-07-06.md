## Title: When you get old, it's something you should keep in mind.
## Date: 2026-07-06
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Maybe this has already been said somewhere, but I really don't like the idea of being called a "toxic old person." There was a time when I was the young upstart, the junior nobody, but I hardly had anyone around me who struck me as that kind of old person. Sure, sometimes you'd see a stranger acting that way in a shop, or hear about examples in other people's stories. But among the people I actually worked with or saw regularly, there was truly no one who made me think, "Now there's a toxic old person." The older people I knew, and the seasoned figures from other circles, all of them listened well to what others had to say. None of them forced their fixed ideas on anyone. They never lectured or scolded, and they never went on about their own accomplishments. The seniors I respected stayed humble no matter how old they got, and I think they were also very good at the art of forgiveness. In TV dramas, you always see the stereotypical overbearing elder wielding power and keeping people down, but the fact that I managed to live so long without really encountering anyone like that in real life may simply mean I've been incredibly lucky.

Having seen so many wonderful older people, I naturally came to feel that I wanted to be like them someday. Even so, I want to say this: I do quietly worry that if I let my guard down, I might become that kind of person myself.

I was once taught that men, if they aren't constantly careful, will inevitably turn stingy and lecherous. And I've spent the last twenty years or so wondering whether, on top of those, getting older means you also risk becoming a nuisance. When people want to attack someone older, all they have to do is call them a "toxic old person" and it sounds perfectly justified. Even the admirable seniors I've known have all had it said about them at one time or another. And sure, they were, each of them, undeniably old.

For now, as far as I can tell about myself, I still find younger people fascinating and worthy of respect, so I think I'm probably okay. But who knows.
