## Title: An exhausting but fun last night.
## Date: 2026-07-09
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

By the way, I really did eat tonkatsu yesterday. It was every bit as delicious as I'd imagined. There's something genuinely wonderful about having tomorrow's pleasure already picked out.

So. Right now, I'm pretty worn out. Like a rag that's been wrung tight and left completely dry.

Last night, I produced a pilot version of something I'd been turning over in my mind for a long time, wondering if it might work. For me, it's actually quite rare to come up with an idea myself and then say, "All right, I'll do it." Mostly because I already know going in that it's going to be exhausting.

To put it simply, it's a live event where I read aloud from a book that people think they've read but actually haven't, and as I go, I insert my own questions and reactions along the way. You might call it a "one-man book club." Taking something I normally do alone, reading, and doing it in front of people who are listening.

N-ta-san said, "So it's kind of like a video game playthrough, but for books." I told him that might not be far off.

While reading, when I think, "Oh, that's lovely," or "How on earth did the author manage to write it this way," I say it out loud. All those things you normally do in silence, alone. You could also call it "storytime for adults."

And one thing that's pretty important: no preparation, no research. I face the book completely bare. In the spirit of something that's been my personal motto lately: "Things only really begin once everyone can see you're a fool." When I do that, the people listening don't have to put on airs either. People who read a lot and people who never read at all, both are welcome. That's the kind of book club this is.

Since it was a pilot, we only streamed it internally, as a test to see whether people would enjoy it. In the end, I was tired but it was genuinely interesting. The response from those who watched was quite positive, so we may end up making it available to the public. Ninety minutes of nonstop reading and talking. Is this really something an old man should be doing?

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi today. The book I chose for the reading was, of all things, by that master of prose, Shiga Naoya.
