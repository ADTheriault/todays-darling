## Title: I have something I'm quietly working hard at.
## Date: 2026-05-10
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

I had planned to just keep at it quietly, but I can't hold it in any longer, so here it is. I bought a brand-new pair of jeans, and I've been wearing them into the ground, trying to break them in until they're fully seasoned. Not pre-distressed. Not someone else's well-worn pair from a vintage shop. I want to wear them myself until they look just right.

Back around the time Hobonichi first launched, I was doing exactly this, in earnest. They grew into something that looked really good, but because I spend so much time sitting at a desk, the seat wore through and got far too thin. I've forgotten a lot of the details from back then, but I think I must have worn them hard for close to a year. The truly devoted types apparently never even wash theirs, but I'm a member of society, more or less, so I washed mine as I went. Even those memories are now a quarter century old.

I own a great many pairs of jeans, so I'd never committed to picking one pair and wearing them day after day. Now that I actually want to start from scratch, it turns out to be surprisingly difficult. It takes real, conscious effort. It's not that I have some particular desire to look stylish, but I'm also not trying to play the stubborn old crank who wears the same jeans every day. I simply felt like doing it. It's probably not so different from thinking, maybe I'll take up fishing, or maybe I'll start running.

This may sound a bit abstract, but the accumulation of each day's hours gets recorded in a single pair of jeans, as creases, as scuffs, as fading, becoming a kind of expression. No one else can buy the same thing, and no one else can make it. And yet here I am, an old man by now, wanting to go out of my way to try this all over again.

I'm about three months in. There are changes, though no one else would notice. It's right around the stage where you might or might not detect the first signs of them starting to look right. Am I bored with it? Of course, I got bored ages ago. But having written about it today, I feel like I can keep going.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi today, as always. I have a feeling I might actually be suited to simple repetition.
