## Title: We are holding a wake for little Kuma.
## Date: 2026-04-26
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

When someone dies, I don't usually talk about it much. If there's someone at the funeral hall holding a microphone, I steer clear, I slip away. I don't want to say anything, and I don't want to write anything either. Eventually, once enough time has passed, I'll remember them, talk about them with others. The time when someone "is here" and the time when someone "was here" are almost continuous, almost no different at all. Saying this or that about it, it's too soon.

But this time, with Kuma-chan, I want to say something now. Because, you know, I was actually planning to go see him on April 19th. I'd heard he wasn't doing well, so I figured once he was out of the hospital I'd head to Nara and, under the pretense of a get-well visit, just shoot the breeze with him. Something like, "So you pulled through again, huh?" "Yeah, you know me." We were going to say things to each other like, "We're both pretty hard to kill, aren't we."

Shinbo said he'd come too, so I thought, well, that's basically going to turn into a continuation of the final installment of "Tasogare." But we wanted to do it, so why not. I'd already started arranging hotels and bullet train tickets.

But then his discharge got pushed back a little, and word came that they'd be continuing treatment. So it became a tentative cancellation, a postponement. Apparently the man himself had said, "We can shoot the breeze in the hospital just fine," but we decided, no, let's wait until he's properly discharged and do it then.

And then what was supposed to be a postponement turned into a cancellation. "Jumping the gun a bit, aren't you," is what I thought. If I could get in touch with the other side right now, that's what I'd tell him. He'd probably answer something like, "More or less on schedule." Well, maybe "more or less," but still, it was a close thing. I wish we could have had at least one more round of nonsense, enough for one more installment of "Tasogare."

I've always thought that when my time comes, I want to be the kind of person whose wake is a lively affair. So that's what I'll do for Kuma-chan. I'll remember all sorts of things, and I'll make sure to laugh a lot. He may be a little ways off now, but I'll fill in the gaps with my imagination and hold a lively, one-man wake.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi today. Watching your friends die, one after another. That, more than anything, is what it means to grow old.
