## Title: What is it that AI lacks?
## Date: 2026-04-21
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

It's a common enough explanation: the difference between AI and humans is that AI has no "body" and no "heart." I don't disagree, but I'd been thinking there must be a better way to put it. This might sound like I'm splitting hairs, but here's what I also think. Something close to a "body" will probably be buildable eventually. Something resembling a "heart" may well emerge in time.

What I've been thinking, in my own completely amateur way, for quite a while now, is this: AI has no "daily life."

Every impressive AI out there has a vast network of information at its disposal, and it can use that network to calculate and store things. We already know it keeps getting smarter. And sure, combine it with robotics and you could probably build an AI capable of physical activity too. But it only does things when there's something to be done. It doesn't eat all sorts of food, or chat about nothing in particular, or wander around aimlessly, or get stopped by someone wanting to talk, or bite into something and think "that's good" or maybe not, or have an unrequited crush, or find itself liked by someone, or get up to use the bathroom, or sit there watching TV that isn't even interesting.

It doesn't live that kind of vague, information-resistant "daily life." It never encounters the accidents that daily life throws up. And it never keeps changing because of those accidents.

Humans, on the other hand, are swimming in a sea of accidents called "daily life." Daily life. In English, the word is "LIFE." And "LIFE" also means the course of your years on earth. More than that, "LIFE" means the very fact of being alive.

When you think of it that way, you could say AI has no "LIFE." And you know what, that makes me glad, somehow, that we humans do have it.

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi again today. Life can be fleeting, and painful too, but it really is something, isn't it.
