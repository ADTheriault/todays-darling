## Title: A place where I can see the one I love.
## Date: 2026-05-20
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

The place where you can meet the people you love is the place those people love. So if you can figure out what someone loves, you can figure out where to find them. Sometimes people who share the same interests meet as strangers and end up finding someone dear to them.

It sounds like I'm laying out the laws of matchmaking for the internet age, but actually, that opening line, "The place where you can meet the people you love," comes from a jingle I wrote when I was around thirty. It came back to me out of nowhere, and I found myself humming it.

It was 1979. A Seiyu supermarket was opening in front of the train station in Saga, and this was the theme for the grand opening campaign. I went to Saga for location scouting and did some interviews, but everyone there just kept saying, "There's nothing here." From our perspective, we kept thinking, "But it's right in front of the station!" And sure enough, there really were very few people around. "Sometimes a biker gang loops around near the station," they told us. But plenty of people also said, "Once the Seiyu opens, I think folks will start coming, little by little." So there was hope.

If "something" (in this case, a Seiyu) appears in a place where people think there's "nothing," people will gather. Well, that's exactly what the company must have been thinking when they decided to build it. That was more or less my line of reasoning when I wrote the copy. If I remember right, it was: "The place where you can meet the people you love." And that became the lyrics for the opening of the jingle.

I can recall what I was thinking nearly fifty years ago as if it were just the other day. And I realize I'm still thinking about the same kinds of things now. It really doesn't change, does it, the stuff people do.

The place where you can meet the people you love is the place those people love. Everything Hobonichi has been doing all along, the recently opened "Hobo no Eki AKAGI," the "Exhibition of the Joys of Everyday Life" that's about to start. It's all the same thing. It's nothing as fancy as "community building" or "regional revitalization." It's just that people who love the same things want to gather together. That's all.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi today, too. Incidentally, that Saga Seiyu closed in 2018.
