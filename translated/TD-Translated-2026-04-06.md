## Title: Not everything needs to be critiqued.
## Date: 2026-04-06
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

I wonder about this. Most people alive on this planet right now, when they eat something, see something, hear something, in most cases they don't sit there and pass judgment on it. And that's true of me, too. I don't go around offering commentary or criticism on every single thing I see, hear, or eat. Personally, I don't think I do it all that much. Well, actually, I can't say I never do it. It does happen. I might even say it happens fairly often... And that's exactly the kind of thing I want to fix, this habit of passing judgment. Whether the verdict is good or bad, why do we feel such a need to judge everything?

And it's not just me. On the internet, enormous numbers of people are constantly passing judgment on things, nonstop. Everyone's turned into one of those characters from a gourmet manga, playing the refined critic. Maybe the whole concept of "looking down from above," that phrase people use now, was invented because someone picked up on this trend.

People do it relentlessly with music, too. I used to think soccer fans were especially prone to it, but these days baseball seems to attract just as much armchair criticism.

Kids, though, they hardly ever pass judgment. If something tastes bad, they either refuse to eat it or force it down. They don't make a fuss about critiquing it. Take them somewhere for fun and if it's boring, they fall asleep. If they're having a good time, they just keep going and never want to stop. Dogs are the same way.

I have a feeling that passing judgment is really a kind of cerebral pleasure. Come to think of it, maybe it's related to the current obsession with "putting things into words." You take an experience and convert it into the symbols of language so you can think about it. That way you can handle more complex ideas and go deeper. You might call it the pleasure of being clever.

Kids, dogs, and me when I'm in a daze, none of us bother with judging anything at all.

Well, I suppose there's room for both ways of being.

Thanks for visiting Hobonichi again today. Personally, I'd like to keep my portion of "passing judgment" small.
