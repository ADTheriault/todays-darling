## Title: I want to bring the hot spring version of me home.
## Date: 2026-06-24
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

For as long as I can remember, I took it as a given that baths were something you took before bed. Work, reading, watching shows, whatever it was, I'd finish everything I wanted to do first, and only when there was nothing left would I go to sleep. I'd kept that routine for decades. So the bath had to come right before bed. It seems bathing was never something I actually wanted to do. Of course it felt good once I got in the water. And keeping your body clean isn't a bad thing. But somehow I'd always thought of it as more of an obligation. So I never really lingered or took my time. I was sleepy, after all. Once I was in the bath, all I wanted was to hurry up and get to bed. It was a stretch of time that pretty much symbolized how little breathing room I had in my life.

But when I go to a hot spring? Completely different person. I'm Mr. Relaxation, king of the bathhouse, lord of the outdoor pool. My mood is expansive, I'll soak again and again, I get all warm and toasty, and I'm savoring every moment. Work can wait, I'll get to it later. The point is to let the hot spring wash away every last bit of fatigue and worry, to sigh as much as I please, and that's perfectly fine. I become an utterly self-indulgent hot spring creature. Isn't it too different from my daily bath at home? It's way too different.

So I thought: I want to bring that hot-spring version of me back to my own house. First, the water temperature at home might have been a touch too lukewarm. Time for a fresh start, turn the heat up a notch. And on top of that, I'd use the Nemukuma Forest bath salts to get those hot-spring minerals in there. Just that alone made a world of difference in how warm and cozy it felt. And then I started actually wanting to enjoy my bath. I thought long and hard about how to make that happen, and I came to the conclusion that "right before bed," the way I'd done it for decades, just wasn't going to work. There was simply no room to breathe.

(A few days of trial and error followed around this point...)

When I switched to bathing around nine or ten at night, somehow it started feeling remarkably like a hot spring. I can even still get work done afterward! And more than anything, I've been able to get to bed a little earlier.

Thanks for coming to Hobonichi again today. Of course, when it comes to tinkering with your daily habits, it's never easy to make things stick.
