## Title: Is it something you look forward to, morning coming when you wake up?

Hmm, let me reconsider for a more natural phrasing:

Do you look forward to morning coming when you wake up?
## Date: 2026-07-08
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

You know when you get into bed, right before you fall asleep, and you think, "When I wake up, morning will be here. I can't wait." Have you ever actually fallen asleep feeling that way? I can imagine it, sure, but I don't think I've ever really experienced it. Well, maybe I have? Maybe I've just forgotten, and there were mornings I actually looked forward to waking up to. If so, I'd love to remember what that felt like. Because wouldn't that be incredible? That feeling of drifting off to sleep excited for morning to come. I bet your face would have a little smile on it. And then you'd slip right into sleep, just like that.

Isn't there some way to try it? What kind of tomorrow would make you look forward to it that much? I can't quite come up with anything, because it's been so long since I've felt that way. Even if it's make-believe, even if it's a joke, I want to know what that tastes like. O grant this old turtle-hearted soul of mine the joy of looking forward to morning.

I mean, it's not like I dread my everyday life or anything. But going to bed looking forward to tomorrow morning, and then waking up with a feeling of genuine happiness. Isn't that more desirable than just about anything? I think "hope" is something that lives inside that feeling. If the word "hope" shows up written in some kind of project proposal, it always feels a little forced, and it's hard to get excited about it.

Come to think of it, I once came up with a line of dialogue. A guy says, "Tomorrow, I'm having tonkatsu." He goes out of his way to tell a coworker during lunch break, in a low voice. He must be so excited about it, just counting the hours. I think that counts as hope, too. I hope he remembers it again right before he falls asleep. Maybe I'll try falling asleep tonight thinking about tomorrow's tonkatsu.

Thanks for coming to Hobonichi again today. Oh, and people who are really into their fan activities, they probably look forward to tomorrow all the time, don't they.
