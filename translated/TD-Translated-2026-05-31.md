## Title: The people you can see in the background of someone giving their all.
## Date: 2026-05-31
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

The phrase "it's all thanks to you" is a common one. It was even the title of a variety show, and you hear and read it all the time as a stock expression. But there are also plenty of times when someone says it and truly means it. In fact, come to think of it, I always mean it sincerely when I say it. Truly, from the bottom of my heart: it's all thanks to everyone.

No one can live without the help of others. When I was young, I often felt like I'd done everything myself. I won't entirely begrudge my younger self that kind of youthful pride, but at the same time, there's a part of me that wants to sit that younger me down and say, "That's not how it works." There was so much that happened thanks to other people, and I'm sure I caused plenty of trouble for them too. This isn't a lesson in morality. It's simply a matter of common sense.

Here's an easy example. A professional baseball manager, if he stepped up to the plate, couldn't hit the opposing pitcher's ball. He can't hit better than any of his players, and if he tried pitching or fielding, he'd be so hopeless he couldn't even make it into the game. No matter who you are, the things you can do on your own are few. Everything beyond that small sliver exists thanks to everyone else.

I mean, look, from the moment a baby comes crying into the world, it survives on nothing but the help of others. You can flush the toilet because someone built the sewage system. After your life is over, someone at the crematorium will take care of your remains. I could go on forever, but it's everything, absolutely everything!

Tomorrow, Monday, marks the start of a full week of "The Exhibition of Living Pleasantly." Behind every one of our teammates working as hard as they can, there are families and people who support them. While moms and dads are giving it their all, their children are waiting patiently at home, and their husbands and wives are holding things together. It's because of all those people that we're able to pour our sweat into this work. I'm genuinely, deeply grateful.

All right, it's the last day of preparation. Safety first, fun second. Let's go!

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi today. The real work begins once you can see just how much you can't do on your own.
