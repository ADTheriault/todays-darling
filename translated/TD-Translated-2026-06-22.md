## Title: If you think about it, society is an open weight class with no mercy, isn't it?
## Date: 2026-06-22
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

In boxing, there are weight classes. They're divided quite finely, and even a small difference in weight can dramatically shift the advantage one way or the other. It's not quite the same thing, but in other sports too, competition is divided by levels: professional, college, high school, middle school, each separated by age and experience. Men and women compete in separate frameworks as well, in consideration of physical differences. The same is true in the world of knowledge and academics. Math competitions, shogi, go: all of them have people competing on fields that are divided to some degree.

But once you step out into the real world, everything becomes an open-weight free-for-all. Age, gender, experience, foundational training: none of it is taken into account. To put it figuratively, an adult twisting a baby's arm, a full-grown man cornering a young person with logic. In the arena of work, none of that raises an eyebrow. Once you've entered the ring, once you've joined the competition, there are no handicaps for differences in raw ability or the size of your organization. Whoever can win, wins. Pour in as much money and manpower as you like, as long as you come out on top. Terrifying, isn't it? Gives you the shivers. Being small, being weak, not having enough of this or that: none of it matters. It's all open weight, no exceptions.

And yet, despite how cruel this competition is, the reason you and I, all of us, have managed to survive is, I think, because we've always had the option of not entering the match. The wise stay away from danger, and so do the weak. Nobody signs up for a fight they know they'll lose. We survive by creating our own weight classes as we go. Someone calls out, "Hey, are you running away?" So what. You don't even owe anyone an explanation for choosing not to participate. Everyone already knows: pick your battles. Think carefully. Only fight where you see a real chance, where you actually want to win.

The world is, of course, an open-weight arena where the strong prevail. But little monkeys and tiny fish are out there doing just fine.

Thanks for visiting Hobonichi again today. The human world has its own version of finding your niche, just like the natural world does.
