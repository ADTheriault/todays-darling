## Title: It's summer, so are you eating somen and stuff?
## Date: 2026-07-20
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

In the morning, not long after waking, I can go without eating anything and feel perfectly fine. So people might take me for someone with a weak appetite. But the truth is, I'll get up, get ready to go out first thing, and head off to eat some ramen I've had my eye on. Apparently, to the boss of the household, this has long been considered "a frightening thing." I only just found this out.

I don't think of ramen as some light little meal. And yet, eating ramen right after waking isn't a strain for me at all. Honestly, I'd be fine with a bowl of grilled eel over rice for breakfast. Tempura over rice, pork cutlet over rice, I never think, "It's morning, better not." Well, I might pass on yakiniku, but if someone said, "There's a great place for morning yakiniku, want to go?" I'd answer, "Morning yakiniku, is that a thing? Sounds good, let's go."

I have absolutely no sense that I've got an iron stomach. But the idea that breakfast should be something light, or that on a hot summer day I ought to have somen noodles, those feelings have almost never come over me. I love light meals, and I love somen too, so I'll eat them gladly, morning or summer heat be damned.

What really exasperated the boss, apparently, was the day we had a reservation at the sushi place, and even though I had a fever, I said "I'm going" and ate just like always. I do remember that. I had a fever and felt a bit unsteady, but the usual delicious sushi was delicious in the usual way.

Come to think of it, back when I was young, I once said that even with a fever I could eat eel, and the president at the time treated me to some. It's not that I have a strong stomach. It's just that what I can eat, I can eat.

It's morning, or it's summer, or the feasts have been piling up, sure, I could trot out those excuses if I wanted to. Society tends to think along those lines, and for the most part I go along with it in my daily life.

But here's a thought. Morning is when you're hungriest, so why not eat whatever you like? And summer wears you out, doesn't it, so shouldn't you build up your strength? All right, all right, I'll go along with it, somen or hiyamugi, whatever you say.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi today too. And when I eat ramen with too much grease in it, I'm also the guy who gets an upset stomach.
