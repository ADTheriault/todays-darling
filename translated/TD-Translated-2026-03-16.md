## Title: Power as a concept.
## Date: 2026-03-16
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Money, beauty, talent of whatever kind: these are all forms of power. Most people wish for more of it. If your power grows, you imagine, surely your freedom will increase. Your possibilities will expand. Your happiness will multiply. Your circle of allies will widen.

But if your power grows, it's just as likely that your constraints will increase too. The things you must not do will multiply. So will your misfortunes. So will the rifts among your allies. That much is easy enough to imagine.

Become a person of importance, and you get to ride in a large car fitted with bulletproof glass. But why, exactly, does it need to be bulletproof? Maybe that tells you everything.

Still, it's too easy to just say you don't need power. If you had almost none, would freedom be there waiting for you? Would possibility? Would happiness? Would anyone be at your side? That, too, is hard to say. Maybe yes. Maybe no.

You can study all the history you like, take in as many stories as you want, and the answer to these questions never becomes clear. Period dramas, gangster films, it's all the same. How to handle power is always the central dilemma. "Just the right amount of power" doesn't make for much of a story. And even when a tale is about ordinary life, power is always lurking in the background.
