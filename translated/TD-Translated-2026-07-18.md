## Title: Wait, ramen, fried rice, and a love like a bouquet.
## Date: 2026-07-18
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

When you're chasing someone, you shout "Stop!" at the person you're after. Of course, shouting "Stop" doesn't mean they'll actually stop, and shouting "Stop" doesn't make you run any faster either. So I found myself wondering all over again whether it might be better not to shout "Stop!", and the answer, in the end, is that you're better off shouting it.

For one thing, it has the effect of announcing to everyone around you the simple fact that "a chase is currently underway." Once you've done that, the person being chased draws attention. It becomes harder for them to slip away and hide, which makes them easier to catch.

And there's one more thing. When someone yells "Stop!" at you, I think it genuinely does increase the odds, even a little, that the person running will actually stop. However determined they are to keep running forever, that's a hard thing to pull off. Sooner or later, somewhere along the way, they might just give up. As a word to coax out that surrender, shouting "Stop!" beats staying silent.

So I've decided that someday, when I find myself chasing after someone who's done something wrong, I'll shout "Stop!" without any embarrassment.

On a related note, what about the way villains in TV dramas say "Die!" as they reach for a weapon? I can't see what effect it's supposed to have, and it only makes the crime and the punishment heavier. Saying it seems to do no good whatsoever.

・Yesterday, I made the "ramen fried rice" I'd promised earlier I would eventually make. Fried rice with the usual ramen toppings mixed in: char siu, bamboo shoots, and green onion. Since it's really just fried rice with ramen elements folded in, it didn't feel especially fancy, but it was delicious. And of course you need egg.

・I finally got around to watching the film "We Made a Beautiful Bouquet." I'd made up my mind, without ever seeing it, that it was probably one of those movies where young people fall in love and then one of them gets sick, so I'd skipped it. It was nothing like that at all. Truly, love really is like a bouquet. I wonder if young people watched this without flinching. They're tough. As for us older folks, we can get through it, though maybe with a few tears.
