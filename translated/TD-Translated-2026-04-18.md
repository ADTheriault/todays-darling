## Title: Today's this and that.
## Date: 2026-04-18
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

So they've settled on "extreme heat day." That's the official name for any day the temperature hits 40 degrees Celsius or above. To think I got to witness the exact moment a name was chosen for something so public, so socially significant. That's kind of amazing. From now on, for the rest of this country's history, any day that reaches 40 degrees or higher will formally be called an "extreme heat day." People can call it a "scorching day" or a "super-sweltering day" if they like, but those are just nicknames. Someone will always correct them: "The official term is extreme heat day." You can call athletic shoes "sneakers" or "sports shoes" or "footgear," whatever you prefer, but for days at 40 degrees and above, only "extreme heat day" is correct.

On a different note. "What am I actually thinking?" I suspect that's the thing I think about most of all. This isn't the same as "finding yourself" or anything like that. It's that I probably don't truly know what I'm thinking. So when I stumble upon some hint, some clue about it, I feel genuinely happy. Or when I figure out the cause or reason behind why I arrived at a particular thought, that makes me happy too. If someone existed who never once questioned what they were thinking, I'd find that person a little troubling, honestly.

On another note entirely, I once resolved, "I'm going to eat natto every day," and I kept at it for a while, but somewhere along the way I stopped. It's not that I came to dislike natto. It's good when I eat it. But I got bored. I couldn't bring myself to love it enough to eat it every single day. "It's very good for you" just wasn't a strong enough reason to keep it up. These experiences of "I tried it and it didn't stick" really do accumulate over time, don't they.

Thanks for visiting Hobonichi again today. Weighing myself every morning and every night, though, that I've kept up all along.
