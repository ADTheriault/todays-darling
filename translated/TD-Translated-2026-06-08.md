## Title: Something like the acknowledgments at the end of a book's afterword.
## Date: 2026-06-08
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

I know I keep writing about the same thing every day, but the truth is, where we are and what we're thinking about has been nothing but the "Life is Fun" exhibition, so forgive me. One week starting June 1st, or ten days if you count setup and construction. We made it to the finish line: "Safety first, fun second." It was fun, wasn't it? Wasn't it fun for you, too?

As usual, I'm going to sing the praises of my people. The crew and the shop staff were all marveling, "I had no idea we'd gotten this strong." But honestly, come on, it's because every one of you thought things through, made changes, and gave it your all this time around. That's what made everything run so smoothly. Even during teardown and the after-party, smiles were blooming everywhere. Even when people were working at incredible speed, there was no shouting, no barking orders. Everyone just moved naturally. I have so much respect for everyone who worked here. You all looked great. More than anything, you looked like you were having fun. When I asked, almost like a greeting, "Did you have fun?" everyone answered instantly, "We had fun!" That made me happy too.

To all the vendors: thank you for boarding this ship with us. And to the part-timers: you were truly wonderful. People who've been with us every time since the first one in Roppongi. Parents and children working together. People who came from far away. People who made friends here. People from Hobonichi's business partners. People who might even be older than me. Former Hobonichi crew members. Mothers who waited for their children to grow up and could finally join. So many of you are already putting in reservations, saying, "I want to do this again next time."

Enormous, enormous gratitude to the families, friends, and neighbors who cheered on the crew working here. To our "landlords" at Sumitomo Realty, to everyone who handled construction, logistics, and security, to Aki Kondo, to Tobi who performed every single day: thank you! And to the Hobonichi Techo team, who held down the fort at our Kanda headquarters the whole time: thank you. And finally, to all the many customers who came and took part: thank you!

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi today, too. This is Itoi, who got home, lay down on the sofa, and immediately got a leg cramp.
