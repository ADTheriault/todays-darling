## Title: These days, age didn't matter, did it.
## Date: 2026-06-01
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

All right, it's time. The show is live. Live as in alive, as in living.

In a real, concrete place called the Shinjuku Sumitomo Building Triangle Plaza, real shops line up, real people gather, and the live event known as "Life's Little Pleasures Exhibition" begins. There's nothing virtual or digital about it. Well, of course digital tools are lending a hand, but everything that happens in that space is live.

The people who've been working pretty hard since the preparation stage were buzzing with excitement, praising each other. Yeah, I get it. The veterans say things like, "Maybe we've actually gotten good at this." Sure, everyone still has their worries, but somehow "everyone other than yourself" looks incredibly dependable. Well then, that's great. We open the doors feeling like we've done everything we could. What happens will happen. Let's keep this energy going.

Even before the event officially started, the live text coverage was already underway. Same as last time, a two-person team going at it full throttle. It only just hit me that those two are more than a generation apart in age. You'd never know it, though. One is senior, the other junior, but age has nothing to do with it.

And when you read the live text coverage, you can see the same thing in every single person who appears, moment by moment. It doesn't matter how old you are, young or old. This is something you really notice when you're doing something concrete like the Life's Little Pleasures Exhibition: the people at Hobonichi might not have ages at all. And no one lords it over anyone else for being higher or lower in the ranks. I thought to myself, genuinely, how comfortable that feels.

An old man like me, neither fussed over with concern nor pushed to the side, just included, welcomed into the fun. I'm truly grateful for that.

This time around, come to think of it, the core of the team running the Life's Little Pleasures Exhibition is made up of people in their twenties. Isn't that the best? I love a company like this.

And so, in good spirits, we're about to welcome Day One. Thank you for visiting Hobonichi today, too. I'm more than a little excited as I greet this day. Hobonichi is the best.
