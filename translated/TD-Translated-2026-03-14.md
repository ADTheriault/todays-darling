## Title: Itoi Shigesato didn't just rave about it — he nearly fainted.
## Date: 2026-03-14
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Well, I completely collapsed the other day. First time in a long while. I was so exhausted I felt dizzy, like I couldn't get enough oxygen, and then I just hit a wall.

So where did this happen, you ask? At the Sanseido Kanda Jimbocho Main Store, of all places. The details were covered in yesterday's Hobonichi live text updates, but basically, while Sanseido was still preparing for their opening, the Hobonichi crew was invited in, and each of us got to buy one book. It was a special little event made possible by neighborly ties and various other connections.

I'd already known about Sanseido's plans for the new store, having attended their announcement events and such. But when those plans actually materialize as a real, physical bookstore, it's something else entirely. I have no reason to flatter them, so I'll just say it honestly: there's real lifeblood running through both the concept and the execution. If this were a film, you'd want the credits to list an executive producer, a director, an art director. You can tell that someone thought deeply about it, and then everyone worked together to bring it to life.

It reminds me, actually, of visiting the Ichikawa Zoological and Botanical Garden recently, the one with Punch the monkey. When you're there, you can just feel how the staff have poured their ingenuity and care into creating that place. It's a similar sensation.

The books seem to be telling you something. "Where did I come from, and where do I want to go?" And that feeling, I think, comes from the editorial sensibility behind how the books are gathered and arranged. My eyes, and then my legs, kept being summoned from one book to the next, and sometimes I'd stop and talk with them. Nothing is lined up in some generic, perfunctory way, so you feel compelled to engage with each one properly.

That is why I was so utterly spent. It was like sitting through an endless series of one-on-one meetings with an enormous number of people. Curiosities, hard questions, confessions, temptations, all coming at me without pause. In a way, it felt like being lost in a forest of distractions. (I can't bring myself to call it something dignified like "a forest of knowledge.") I was truly overwhelmed. I should have braced myself better before going in.

So next time, I'm going back as a regular customer, properly prepared for a rematch.

Thank you, as always, for visiting Hobonichi today. I made careful note of every book I plan to buy with my own money next time.
