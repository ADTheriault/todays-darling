## Title: More important than important things.
## Date: 2026-02-23
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

"When you think you're doing something good, it's best to think you're doing something a little bit bad. That keeps things about right." These are words from Yoshimoto Takaaki. I can't remember when I first heard them, but as time passes, I've come to think this is truly important. It sounds like a paradox, so very typical of Yoshimoto, but now, looking back, I realize it's very close to Shinran's teaching: "Even the virtuous can attain rebirth, how much more so the wicked."

"Good things," "big things," "important things" can really intoxicate people. And so people rush headlong toward "important things" and "good things," without a glance at "bad things" or "small things." I think that's where things go wrong.

Before I'd ever met Yoshimoto, I was reading an essay by Tomioka Taeko, and came across a passage where she wrote something like how she'd sometimes see Yoshimoto Takaaki in the evening, carrying a shopping basket. The Yoshimoto Takaaki of "Theory of Shared Illusion," making dinner. The poet Tomioka Taeko silently watching him in the twilight of downtown Tokyo. Later, I would come to see for myself that this was indeed simply fact, just as it was, but even when I was around twenty, I found it beautiful.

Also, this might be just hearsay, but there's a story that Akashiya Sanma once said about Kimura Takuya: "Kimura's properly being a father, that's great." I completely agree, and I also think Sanma is great for saying it. Kimura would probably reply with a puzzled look, "But I actually am a father, so what's so great about it?" Well, yes, but that's exactly what's so important. It's something that seems like anyone could do, but not everyone can.
