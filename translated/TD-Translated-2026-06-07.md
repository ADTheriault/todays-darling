## Title: Alright, year 29 has begun.
## Date: 2026-06-07
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

As of today, Hobonichi has entered its 29th year. Yesterday, June 6th, was the 28th anniversary of our founding, which means the very next day we're already into year 29. I wonder if everyone busy working at the "Life's Little Pleasures Exhibition" even knows. Hey, year 29 has begun!

But really, if someone asked, "What kind of company is Hobonichi?" I'm not sure even we could give a clear answer. Isn't that a problem, though? You're supposed to know what you're doing so you can buckle down and do it. You're supposed to have a goal so you can work backward and figure out what needs doing now. Nobody ever really nagged us about that kind of thing, though. Maybe they figured there was no point. But even without being told, we've managed just fine.

Seeds get planted in an "open lot of ideas" that isn't the same as everyone else's. Those seeds, through sun and clouds and rain, sometimes grow tall and sometimes don't. The leaves photosynthesize, flowers bloom, roots drink up water and nutrients from deep underground, and if things go well, fruit appears. But if you fixate too much on the fruit as the purpose, you end up chasing nothing but that. What matters more is the process, wondering what will grow, noticing how beautiful it is, finding it fascinating. That process is what nurtures us, the ones doing the nurturing.

And having been nurtured, we go out with energy to find another "open lot of ideas" and plant more seeds. Maybe a field takes shape. Maybe a forest. It's not just us, the people, who grow. The place itself grows. Bees come buzzing, birds arrive, animals wander in. Follow a rabbit and you might find a mysterious hole. It's wonderful. And because it's wonderful, you can play with everything you've got. You end up completely exhausted, and you look around at your companions and think, I'm so glad they're here.

Well then, today is the final day of the "Life's Little Pleasures Exhibition." Thank you, as always, for being here with Hobonichi. I think AI is amazing, but I'd like to believe that people are even more so.
