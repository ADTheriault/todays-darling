## Title: But honestly, I don't really care either way.
## Date: 2026-05-02
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Realistically, we're eating at night, so I suppose there's no helping it if people call dinner "night-meal" instead of "evening-meal." Families with small children might serve dinner on the early side to get them to bed sooner, but even then, if it's six-thirty or seven o'clock, you could already call that night. Still, I plan to keep saying "evening-meal" for at least a few more years. Oh, and what about the question you get at a traditional inn: "What time would you like your evening meal?" That's probably not going to change. After all, there's also the late-night snack, so you can't go using up the word "night" too early.

On an entirely different note, very few people actually pronounce the Japanese word for "queen" correctly. The word is a compound of "woman" and "king," but somewhere along the way an extra syllable crept in, and almost everyone mispronounces it with the sounds in the wrong order. To their credit, NHK announcers are careful to say it properly. Maybe they have special training for that sort of thing.

Speaking of which, and this is a continuation of the same kind of thing, most people seem to slur the pronunciation of "NHK" itself. When there's an earthquake and someone wants to check the emergency news, they'll say something like "Put on NHK," but with a lazy, compressed version of the letters. Or maybe that's just how it sounds to me. It does seem like very few people enunciate each letter crisply. At this point, I think that's just how it goes, and that's fine.

That said, there are cases where it's probably not fine. For instance, when people mean "to go on and on endlessly," they'll sometimes use the word for "eternity" instead of the correct word, which means something like "at great length." The two are close enough in feeling, both suggesting something that stretches on and on, so I can understand the mix-up. Maybe in two hundred years or so, using "eternity" that way will be officially accepted.

Anyway, I just felt like writing down some things I don't usually talk about. Thank you, as always, for visiting Hobonichi today. And for the record, my own Japanese is far from proper.
