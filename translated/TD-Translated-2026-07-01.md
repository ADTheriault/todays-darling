## Title: Being connected, and being alone.
## Date: 2026-07-01
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

The title of yesterday's piece was "I'm sure it'll be: 'That was great! That was great!'" No matter how the soccer match against Brazil turned out, I wanted to be able to say "That was great! That was great!" It wasn't meant as a prediction. I wrote it as a wish. There's maybe a touch of putting on a brave face in there, but I think "That was great! That was great!" was the right call.

Why is it so much fun, all of us watching a live sporting event together? I imagine it's because you can feel that you're "connected to so many people," that "so many people are feeling the same way you are." Especially late at night, when you'd normally be alone in your everyday life, there's something deeply satisfying about feeling that "all these people are connected to me right now." If you can think, "Look how many of us there are," it gives you courage just to be alive. Maybe that's how alone we usually are.

Being in a crowd at a live concert, attending a rally where everyone shares the same conviction, being in the middle of a fireworks show or a festival. We play it cool and say things like, "Sure is crowded, huh," but we went out of our way to be there, so clearly we don't mind. Just knowing that there are this many people like you, I think it awakens some very primal pleasure.

I talk about this as though I'm just an observer, but I do love "being connected to lots of people." Sometimes people tease me, calling me "Mr. Majority." And yet, honestly, it's a little more complicated than that. In those moments of "being connected to lots of people," I sometimes get wrapped in a different kind of loneliness, not the same as when I'm truly alone. I'm not sure how to put it. It's like wanting to let go of the hands I'm holding in this enormous human chain, just for a moment. Like wanting to test whether I could let go anytime I wanted.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi again today. People's feelings really do come as one, front and back inseparable.
