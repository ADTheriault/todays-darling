## Title: Are you finding something you can lose yourself in?
## Date: 2026-05-05
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Being completely absorbed in something... it really is a wonderful thing. If you're caught up in something right now, that time is a treasure. Please, hold on to it. Though honestly, what might be even more amazing is when you're so absorbed that the question of whether you're absorbed or not doesn't even cross your mind.

When children are lost in play, it's like they become one with whatever they're doing. You tell them, "All right, that's enough now," and they don't hear a word of it.

Music, manga, games, sports. Honestly, I think it doesn't matter what it is.

I started reading a book once and it got so interesting that I secretly kept reading until the middle of the night. I have never, ever forgotten that. It was Kita Morio's *Doctor Manbo's Book of Insects*.

An elementary school kid staying up until the small hours is obviously not great for their health, and you wouldn't want it to become a habit. Books aren't going anywhere. You can slip in a bookmark and pick it up again the next day. And a responsible adult is right to tell a child exactly that.

Still, the feeling I had that night is something I count among my greatest possessions. The short hand of the clock had moved to a place it never usually reached while I was awake. I was lying in bed, unable to stop reading. I don't remember much beyond that. I probably didn't finish the book that night. "The middle of the night" was most likely one in the morning at the latest. Even so, that experience of being completely absorbed was enormously important to me.

As an adult, sure, I've lost track of time with games, TV dramas, and sometimes even work. And right now, more than ever, I feel a strong urge to affirm all of it, every last instance.

My own capacity for that kind of total absorption has grown softer somewhere along the way.

Thank you for visiting Hobonichi today. Better than learning is enjoying. Better than enjoying is playing. That goes for grown-ups too.
