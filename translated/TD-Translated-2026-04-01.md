## Title: Confessions of a nail clipper enthusiast.
## Date: 2026-04-01
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

I've thought about nail clippers for a long time. What kind is best? Domestic, imported. I've bought more than I can count. Cutting, testing, using them day after day, recommending them to people, sometimes just giving them away, buying multiples of the same one, declaring, "This is the best nail clipper in the world." And even then, I never stopped searching, thinking there might be something even better out there. Because of this, my reigning "best nail clipper" has changed hands several times, like a championship title passing from one holder to the next. Always searching, always on the lookout, like a song that never quite resolves.

A good nail clipper has sharp, keen blades. And since you're placing your nail between two sharp blades and snapping through it, wouldn't the blades just collide and destroy each other's edge? I did wonder about that, but no, that's not how it works. The blades don't crash head-on. They're designed to slide past each other with the slightest offset, so what's happening really is cutting. Anyway, that's the kind of thing I was chasing: the tool itself, the nail clipper as an object.

But then, just recently, this late in the game, I found myself questioning not the tool but the technique. "Have I been doing this wrong?"

You see, I've always cut my nails short. Deep. Looking at my fingers from the palm side, you'd never see any nail peeking past the fingertip. And I always cut them to follow the curve of the fingertip, shaping them like an arc: "(" . That was my method.

But then I learned there's a school of thought that says this is all wrong. The arc is no good. If anything, you should be going for a straight edge: "[" . Don't cut into the sides, they say. Cut straight across. Wait, what? I've been doing the deep-curved-arc thing for three quarters of a century now, not to brag or anything. Part of me wanted to dig in my heels, but I am, after all, a seeker on the path of the nail clipper. So off I went, without delay, to buy a straight-edge clipper.

I tried it. It's terrible. It gets in the way of daily life. The corners of my nails catch on everything, snagging against the world in the most unnatural way. Maybe I just need to get used to it, but it's been about two weeks now and I'm still struggling. I'm on the verge of giving up on the "[" approach, but my spirit of inquiry won't let me quit just yet, so I went ahead and bought a nipper-style clipper that also cuts straight.

Thank you, as always, for visiting Hobonichi today. I have to be honest, though. After a little more experimenting, I'm almost certainly going back to my old rounded, deep-trimmed nails.
