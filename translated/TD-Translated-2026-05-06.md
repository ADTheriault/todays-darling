## Title: When moods feel alike.
## Date: 2026-05-06
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

Yesterday, I got to see Moegara at the "Kayō Kissa" event, so I went ahead and told him something I'd been dying to say. That the main character of the Korean drama I'm watching most intently right now, "Everyone Is Fighting Their Own Worthlessness," reminds me of him. It's not exactly that their faces look alike. If you put their photos side by side, they might not resemble each other at all. Actually, when I just now searched for a photo of "Ku Gyohwan," who plays the lead, and compared it with one of Moegara, they're not entirely unlike each other. And come to think of it, Masayoshi Yamazaki looks somewhat similar too. The face and hairstyle may be part of it, of course, but more than anything, it's the mood that's similar. Something about unspoken preferences, or how they want to exist in this world.

Moegara hasn't seen the drama, so naturally he neither agreed nor disagreed, but I really do want him to watch it. The only trouble is, in the first episode, the main character, Hwang Dong-man, appears as an incredibly obnoxious guy, so it might just come across as "Moegara at his absolute worst." I can't really blame him if he takes it that way. Even I started watching with some distance, thinking, "If someone like this were actually in my life, it'd be a real problem."

Anyway, I think it's the writing that makes the show so good. Let me pick out a scene the main character isn't even in. For example, there's a moment where a married couple, both minor characters, are having an argument. The husband, a film director, gets angry: "Do you even love me?" His wife, who also happens to be the head of the production company and therefore his boss, fires back: "Twenty years of marriage, and you're asking about love? Saying 'no' wouldn't hurt me. Saying 'yes' wouldn't do me any good. Think it over carefully." The husband is left completely off balance.

I've taken the thread of our conversation at "Kayō Kissa" and continued it right here. Thank you for visiting Hobonichi again today. And just like that, Golden Week draws to a close.
