## Title: Persistently, about "things I don't understand."
## Date: 2026-02-25
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

In my Hobonichi 5-Year Planner, on February 23rd, 2024, two years ago, I wrote: "It's not that I'm abandoning reason, but I wonder if things we 'understand' no longer hold any power. Only things we 'don't understand,' yet can't look away from, are the things that grow."

I've been thinking about that ever since, for these past two years. Now, two years later, ChatGPT has become completely commonplace, and that feeling has only grown stronger.

I understand that things we can explain as meaning form the basis of communication. Society itself is surely built on that premise.

What exactly did I imagine two years ago when I used the phrase "things we don't understand"? Now I don't even know that anymore.

But now, looking at it again, what if I tried substituting something like "a person you love" into that phrase?

Even if we could fully clarify the reasons we love someone, the value of loving them, the meaning of it all, so what? Probably the feeling of loving someone you love doesn't need to be understood, and there's no point in understanding it in greater detail.

"Things we don't understand," yet can't look away from: to keep our eyes fixed on them just as they are, that's what it means to nurture something.

Or something like that, I say casually, but you know, "things we don't understand" tend to get treated as if they're "as good as nothing."

"Things we don't understand" get reframed as "things we'll understand eventually" or "things we should really be able to understand," and when they're neatly explained that way, people go, "Oh, now I get it," and accept them.

But even if they're accepted, it doesn't really matter. In fact, all the wonderful things are "things we don't understand."

Well, thanks for reading this far. Thank you for coming to Hobonichi today. All I can do is write with faith that you'll come and read.
