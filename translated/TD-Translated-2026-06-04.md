## Title: Sailing on the ship called "Hobonichi."
## Date: 2026-06-04
## Author: Shigesato Itoi
## Content:

This goes back a long time. Probably about twenty-eight years ago. I realized that the way we worked could be likened to a ship.

Back then, organizations, companies, were thought of in terms of tree diagrams, tournament brackets, triangles with the all-important apex at the top. But what if you tipped that triangle on its side and laid it flat? Then there's no more top and bottom, just front and back. The whole thing starts to look like a ship, moving forward together. That was the image I had.

On a ship, every person aboard matters. Deciding which direction to go is important, and so is watching the weather, running the engine, loading and unloading cargo, cooking the meals, which is to say, peeling the potatoes. Everyone's role is essential, and you have to look out for one another or the whole thing falls apart. There's an old saying about how the ocean floor lies just on the other side of the hull. If the ship goes down, you all become flotsam.

I'm not trying to make everything sound life-or-death, but I liked the idea that we're all crew on the same ship, working together. Even if we happen to be on dry land. That's when I started using the word "crew."

It's not the most conventional way to put things, so I feel a little self-conscious about it and try not to overdo it. But when the moment feels right, I still say "crew." It's just "crew," after all.

Day to day, the crew members work in their own specialized areas. But when something comes up, they pitch in on things outside their expertise. As individual people, they do what they can to help each other. We're on a ship, so there's no other way, and mutual support is something everyone signed on for when they came aboard.

I think the people who joined Hobonichi recently and the ones who've been crew here for a long time probably share that same feeling. If you're on this ship, it's the same even for part-timers.

And come to think of it, at the "Life Is Fun" exhibition, I have a feeling the customers, the passengers, are on the same ship too.

Thank you for coming to Hobonichi again today. This ship weathered a typhoon yesterday. We're sailing on, and enjoying the voyage.
